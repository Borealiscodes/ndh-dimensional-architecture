# ⭐ **2. The Core Idea: Stability Has Two Forms**

### **Stability (Static)**  
The calm, unmoving equilibrium of a dimensional field.

### **Stadility (Dynamic)**  
> **Stadility — stability in motion; equilibrium under active correction.**

Stadility is the NDH term for *living stability* — the system’s ability to stay coherent even while things are changing.

If stability is a lake, stadility is a river.

For cultural completeness, NDH humor canon states:

> “If it can oscillate, it can stabilize;  
> if it can stabilize, it can DimmaDome.”  
> — Doug DimmaDome, Owner of the Dimmsdale DimmaDome (honorary patron of NDH equilibrium)

# ⭐ **4. Drift & Continuity: The Two Forces**

### **Drift**  
The destabilizing force.  
Anything that pushes a field away from equilibrium.

### **Continuity**  
The stabilizing force.  
Anything that restores coherence and flow.

NDH stability is the dance between these two.

For the full mechanics, see the **Continuity Map**.

# ⭐ **5. SID: The Stability Intervention Directive**  
SID is NDH’s correction engine — the system that steps in when drift gets too strong.

SID has three modes:

- **AFL** — emergent correction (lower fields)  
- **CLB** — stabilization correction (mid fields)  
- **DSR** — realization correction (upper fields)  

SID is the “governor” of stability.

For full details, open the **Dynamics**.

# ⭐ **6. How Stability Moves (Dynamics)**  
Stability isn’t static — it moves, oscillates, resonates, and sometimes destabilizes.

NDH defines four dynamic states:

- **Stable** — calm  
- **Oscillatory** — wobbling  
- **Resonant** — amplifying  
- **Perturbed** — unstable  

SID intervenes to restore stadility.

The visual companion is the **Dynamics PNG**, referenced in the Dynamics document.

# ⭐ **7. How Fields Interact (Interaction Model)**  
Dimensional fields don’t exist alone — they influence each other.

There are four interaction types:

- **Collision** — drift meets drift  
- **Merge** — continuity meets continuity  
- **Bifurcation** — continuity splits  
- **Cross‑Dimensional Influence** — higher fields regulate lower ones  

This is the “behavioral layer” of stability.
