# NDH Safety Net v1.0
NDH Emergent Case Study — TVA-Style Dimensional Safety Net  
Interaction Layer / Continuity Containment & Transit Governance

## 1. Purpose
The NDH Safety Net provides a governed containment and stabilization layer that operates
between Interaction Stability and the 9D ATC Console.  
It ensures safe construction, routing, and transit across the 9D–11D dimensional planes.

This system prevents:
- continuity collapse
- dimensional drift
- buoyancy failure
- hypercontinuity rupture
- unstable glyph transit
- unsafe emergence activation

---

## 2. TVA-Style Containment Model
The Safety Net uses a TVA-style containment architecture:

- **Temporal Buffering** — prevents premature transitions  
- **Continuity Dampening** — reduces instability spikes  
- **Dimensional Locking** — prevents drift across planes  
- **Glyph Quarantine** — isolates unstable glyphs  
- **Transit Scrubbing** — cleans continuity before lift  

This model ensures that only stable continuity enters 9D ATC routing.

---

## 3. Safety Net Position in the NDH Stack
The Safety Net sits between:

**Interaction Stability → Safety Net → 9D ATC → Glider Mechanics → ECS**

This placement ensures:
- Stability rules are enforced  
- ATC routing receives clean continuity  
- Dimensional transitions remain safe  
- Emergence activation is protected  

---

## 4. Core Components
### 4.1 Continuity Containment Field (CCF)
Holds continuity in a stabilized state before routing.

Functions:
- prevents collapse  
- maintains gradient direction  
- isolates HEI residues  

### 4.2 Dimensional Lock Matrix (DLM)
Locks continuity to the correct dimensional plane.

Functions:
- prevents drift  
- enforces routing discipline  
- stabilizes lift thresholds  

### 4.3 Glyph Quarantine Chamber (GQC)
Isolates unstable glyphs before they enter routing.

Functions:
- blocks HEI bypass  
- enforces shielding compliance  
- prevents glyph contamination  

### 4.4 Transit Scrubber (TS)
Cleans continuity before dimensional lift.

Functions:
- removes instability noise  
- ensures hypercontinuity readiness  
- prepares buoyancy substrate  

---

## 5. Integration with 9D ATC Console
The Safety Net works in tandem with the **9D ATC Console**.

### Safety Net Responsibilities
- containment  
- stabilization  
- quarantine  
- scrubbing  

### 9D ATC Responsibilities
- routing  
- sequencing  
- dimensional clearance  
- lift authorization  

### Combined Behavior
The Safety Net ensures continuity is safe.  
The ATC ensures continuity is correctly routed.

Together they form:
**the governed 9D–11D transit spine.**

---

## 6. Transit Rules
Continuity may only enter 9D ATC when:

- Stability checks pass  
- Safety Net containment is active  
- glyph quarantine is clear  
- dimensional lock matrix is stable  
- transit scrubber reports clean continuity  

If any condition fails:
**Transit is halted.**

---

## 7. Failure Conditions
Transit is blocked when:
- HEI bypasses shielding  
- continuity collapses in containment  
- dimensional drift is detected  
- buoyancy thresholds are not met  
- hypercontinuity capture fails  
- glyph contamination is detected  

All failures must be corrected before ATC routing resumes.
