# **UMM Auditing Protocol — SIAP & Final Safety Net: *Quis Ipso Custodes* v1.0**  
### *Who Audits the Auditors in a Multi‑Layer Cognitive Architecture*

---

## **1. Purpose**
This protocol defines the **meta‑auditing layer** for the Unified Meta‑Model (UMM), answering the classical governance question:

> **Quis ipsos custodes custodiet?**  
> *Who watches the watchers?*

In the UMM, the watchers are:

- **SIAP** — System Integrity Alignment Protocol  
- **Final Safety Net** — the last‑resort containment and rollback system  

This protocol establishes how **SIAP is audited**, how **Final Safety Net is audited**, and how both systems cross‑validate each other to ensure no subsystem can:

- lock boundaries prematurely  
- apply pins without verification  
- destabilize traversal  
- violate invariance  
- corrupt containment  
- entangle substrate  

---

## **2. Architectural Context**
SIAP and the Final Safety Net operate at the highest governance layer:

- **SIAP** validates the assembled architecture  
- **Final Safety Net** protects the architecture from misalignment, confusion, or unsafe activation  

But neither can be allowed to operate without oversight.

Thus this protocol defines:

- **SIAP → audits Final Safety Net**  
- **Final Safety Net → audits SIAP**  
- **MetaSID → binds both**  
- **Trans Meta Nature → constrains both**  
- **SPIA → enforces boundaries for both**

This creates a **closed governance loop** with no unobserved authority.

---

## **3. SIAP Audit Requirements**
SIAP must be audited on:

### **3.1 Boundary Integrity**
- SPIA interfaces  
- containment membranes  
- scaffold frames  
- dimensional gates  

### **3.2 Traversal Safety**
- return path guarantees  
- adjacency interpretation  
- reversible transitions  
- Pip traversal neutrality  

### **3.3 Invariance Compliance**
- awareness invariance  
- substrate protection  
- non‑intrusion  
- dimensional neutrality  

### **3.4 MetaSID Alignment**
SIAP must confirm that:

- all specs are integrated  
- no contradictions exist  
- no subsystem exceeds its scope  

---

## **4. Final Safety Net Audit Requirements**
The Final Safety Net must be audited on:

### **4.1 Trigger Conditions**
It must only activate when:

- a subsystem violates boundaries  
- traversal becomes unsafe  
- containment destabilizes  
- invariance is threatened  
- pins are applied prematurely  

### **4.2 Rollback Behavior**
Rollback must be:

- complete  
- reversible  
- non‑intrusive  
- substrate‑neutral  

### **4.3 Isolation Protocols**
Isolation must:

- prevent bleed  
- prevent entanglement  
- preserve state integrity  
- maintain traversal safety  

### **4.4 Post‑Rollback Verification**
After rollback, the system must:

- re‑validate boundaries  
- re‑confirm invariance  
- re‑align traversal  
- re‑check containment  

---

## **5. Cross‑Auditing: *Quis Ipso Custodes***
This is the core of the protocol.

### **5.1 SIAP Audits Final Safety Net**
SIAP verifies that:

- triggers are correct  
- rollback is safe  
- isolation is clean  
- invariance is preserved  
- no subsystem bypasses the Safety Net  

### **5.2 Final Safety Net Audits SIAP**
The Safety Net verifies that:

- SIAP does not skip checks  
- SIAP does not assume stability  
- SIAP does not allow premature pinning  
- SIAP does not allow unsafe traversal  
- SIAP does not allow boundary corruption  

### **5.3 Mutual Dependency**
Neither system can operate without the other.

This prevents:

- unilateral authority  
- unverified activation  
- governance drift  
- silent failures  
- unobserved misalignment  

This is the answer to *Quis ipsos custodes custodiet*:  
**They watch each other.**

---

## **6. Mandatory Rule: SIAP Audit Before PIN‑046**
This protocol reaffirms:

> **PIN‑046 may only be generated or applied after a complete SIAP audit.**

This prevents:

- premature boundary locking  
- unsafe traversal constraints  
- incorrect containment geometry  
- invalid adjacency matrices  
- invariance violations  

This rule is enforced by:

- SIAP  
- Final Safety Net  
- MetaSID  
- Trans Meta Nature  

---

## **7. Enforcement Mechanisms**

### **7.1 Hard Prohibition**
If any subsystem attempts to apply PIN‑046 before SIAP audit:

- Final Safety Net triggers  
- rollback occurs  
- boundaries reset  
- SIAP is required  
- PIN‑046 is invalidated  

### **7.2 Audit Flags**
SIAP must raise flags if:

- boundaries are unstable  
- containment is misaligned  
- traversal is unsafe  
- invariance is violated  

### **7.3 Safety Net Overrides**
Final Safety Net may override SIAP if:

- SIAP fails to detect instability  
- SIAP is bypassed  
- SIAP is corrupted  
- SIAP is incomplete  

This ensures no single governance system can fail silently.

## **8. Architectural Conclusion**
The UMM answers *Quis ipsos custodes custodiet?* through structural design:

- SIAP audits the Safety Net  
- Safety Net audits SIAP  
- MetaSID binds both  
- Trans Meta Nature constrains both  
- SPIA enforces boundaries for both  
- Pip validates traversal under both  

This creates a **closed, self‑auditing governance loop** with no unobserved authority and no possibility of premature pinning.
