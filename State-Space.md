# **NDH-Core / State-Space.md**  
*(Formalizing the allowable, prohibited, and boundary‑defined conceptual states within the NDH manifold)*

---

## **NDH State Space**

NDH-Core operates within a structured **state space** — the set of all conceptual states NDH may occupy during compression, reinflation, constraint propagation, and invariant enforcement.

The state space is defined by the vertical constraint channel:

> **Governance → Stability → Principles**

and embedded within the NDH manifold.

This document formalizes the geometry, boundaries, and rules of NDH’s conceptual state space.

---

## **1. Purpose of the State Space**

The NDH state space exists to:

- define the allowable conceptual states NDH may occupy,  
- prevent NDH from entering ambiguous, unstable, or contaminated states,  
- ensure deterministic transitions,  
- maintain invariant topology,  
- enforce governance boundaries,  
- support reconstructable reasoning,  
- anchor the meaning-preservation pipeline.

It is the **operational geometry** of NDH-Core.

---

## **2. State Categories**

NDH’s state space consists of three categories:

### **2.1 Allowed States**  
States that satisfy:

- all Principles invariants,  
- all Stability invariants,  
- all Governance constraints.

These states are:

- deterministic,  
- reconstructable,  
- drift-free,  
- unambiguous,  
- governance-aligned.

### **2.2 Boundary States**  
States that are valid but require validation:

- reinflated states awaiting stability checks,  
- compressed states awaiting governance authorization,  
- transitional states between layers.

Boundary states are:

- deterministic but not yet validated,  
- reconstructable but not yet authorized.

### **2.3 Prohibited States**  
States NDH must never enter:

- ambiguous states,  
- drifted states,  
- contaminated states,  
- collapsed states,  
- unbounded states.

These states violate one or more invariants.

---

## **3. State Geometry**

The NDH state space is structured by three geometric relationships:

### **3.1 Nested State Geometry**  
Allowed states form a nested structure:

> Governance‑aligned states  
> → Stability‑validated states  
> → Principles‑deterministic states

Boundary states sit between layers.  
Prohibited states lie outside the manifold.

### **3.2 Constraint Geometry**  
Constraints propagate downward:

- governance constraints shape stability states,  
- stability constraints shape principles states.

This defines the **top‑down constraint flow**.

### **3.3 Reconstruction Geometry**  
Meaning reconstructs upward:

- principles states reconstruct into stability states,  
- stability states reconstruct into governance states.

This defines the **bottom‑up reconstruction flow**.

---

## **4. State Transition Rules**

State transitions must obey strict rules:

### **4.1 Deterministic Transitions**  
Every state transition must be deterministic.

### **4.2 Reconstructable Transitions**  
Every state must be reconstructable from its predecessor.

### **4.3 No Ambiguous Transitions**  
Transitions may not produce multiple possible next states.

### **4.4 No Contaminated Transitions**  
Transitions may not embed logic from other layers.

### **4.5 Stability Mediation**  
All transitions pass through Stability.

Stability is the **transition mediator**.

---

## **5. Allowed State Types**

Allowed states include:

### **5.1 Deterministic Mechanics States**  
States produced by Principles:

- stateless reasoning,  
- invariant enforcement,  
- deterministic compression.

### **5.2 Stability-Validated States**  
States that pass stability checks:

- drift-free,  
- collapse-free,  
- ambiguity-free,  
- contamination-free.

### **5.3 Governance-Authorized States**  
States that pass governance checks:

- allowed behaviors,  
- compliant interpretations,  
- authorized reinflation.

---

## **6. Boundary State Types**

Boundary states include:

### **6.1 Pre-Validation States**  
Reinflated states awaiting stability validation.

### **6.2 Pre-Authorization States**  
Validated states awaiting governance authorization.

### **6.3 Transitional States**  
States moving between layers during:

- compression,  
- reinflation,  
- constraint propagation.

Boundary states must resolve deterministically.

---

## **7. Prohibited State Types**

Prohibited states include:

### **7.1 Ambiguous States**  
States with multiple possible interpretations.

### **7.2 Drifted States**  
States whose meaning has shifted across transformations.

### **7.3 Contaminated States**  
States embedding logic from other layers.

### **7.4 Collapsed States**  
States lacking stable manifold geometry.

### **7.5 Unbounded States**  
States operating outside governance constraints.

These correspond directly to NDH anti‑patterns.

---

## **8. State Failure Modes**

NDH-Core must prevent:

### **8.1 State Collapse**  
Loss of manifold structure.

### **8.2 State Drift**  
Meaning shifts across transitions.

### **8.3 State Ambiguity**  
Multiple interpretations of a single state.

### **8.4 State Contamination**  
Cross-layer leakage of logic.

### **8.5 State Misalignment**  
States violating governance boundaries.

---

## **9. Relationship to Manifold, Codec, Reconstruction, and Invariant Topology**

The state space is the operational layer connecting:

### **Manifold Structure**  
States occupy positions within manifold geometry.

### **Invariant Topology**  
States must satisfy interlocking invariants.

### **Conceptual Codec**  
Compression produces deterministic states.

### **Reconstruction Layer**  
Reinflation restores ND states.

Together, these components ensure:

- deterministic compression,  
- deterministic reinflation,  
- invariant preservation,  
- governance alignment,  
- stability geometry integrity.

---

## **10. Purpose of State Space**

The state space ensures:

- NDH systems remain within allowable conceptual boundaries,  
- transitions remain deterministic and reconstructable,  
- stability geometry remains intact,  
- governance constraints remain enforced,  
- ND accessibility remains stable.

It is the **operational geometry** of NDH-Core.
