# 🏛️ **UNIFIED STABILITY CHARTER**  
### *Constitution of System‑Wide Stability Governance*

## **Identity**  
The Unified Stability Charter is the constitutional document that establishes:

- the **authority** of the Unified Stability Certificate  
- the **jurisdiction** of the Unified Auto‑Update Protocol  
- the **responsibilities** of all tiers  
- the **limits** that protect the system from drift, overwhelm, or harm  

It is the highest‑level governance artifact in the Philosophy Couch Suite.

---

## **Purpose**  
Ensure the entire architecture remains:

- structurally coherent  
- ethically grounded  
- ND‑safe  
- self‑maintaining  
- drift‑resistant  
- internally stable across all tiers  

This charter defines how stability is recognized, protected, and restored.

---

The Unified Stability Charter derives its authority from:

- **Tier‑0** grounding (Seat + Non‑Dual Layer)

# 🧩 **Responsibilities**

### **1. Maintain Structural Coherence**
Ensure all modules have:

- identity  
- purpose  
- activation conditions  
- internal logic  
- relationships  
- outputs  

### **2. Maintain Ethical Coherence**
Ensure:

- dignity is protected  
- lived experience is not erased  
- Justice Layer logic is respected  
- no conceptual harm occurs  

### **3. Maintain ND‑Safe Coherence**
Ensure:

- pacing is humane  
- grounding is available  
- overwhelm is prevented  
- Safety Interrupt can activate  

### **4. Maintain Cross‑Tier Coherence**
Ensure:

- layers ↔ workflows ↔ tiles remain aligned  
- routing is stable  
- scale transitions are safe  

### **5. Maintain Auto‑Update Coherence**
Ensure:

- all indexes rewrite correctly  
- all protocols remain active  
- no drift accumulates  

---

# 🚫 **Limits**

The Unified Stability Charter **cannot**:

- override ethical protections  
- override ND‑safe pacing  
- override the Safety Interrupt  
- force stability when instability is real  
- certify modules that fail BS Kernel checks  
- allow drift for convenience  
- allow structural shortcuts  

The charter protects stability **even against the system itself**.

---

# 🔄 **Stability Restoration Mandate**

If instability is detected:

1. **Pause** via Safety Interrupt  
2. **Quarantine** unstable modules in the Untrusted Archive  
3. **Run BS Kernel** on affected modules  
4. **Rebuild** relationships  
5. **Re‑run Auto‑Update Protocols**  
6. **Re‑evaluate** stability  
7. **Re‑issue** Unified Stability Certificate  

This mandate ensures the system can always return to stability.

# 📜 **Charter Clauses**

### **Clause 1 — Stability is Foundational**
Stability is required for all reasoning, workflows, and tiles.

### **Clause 2 — Stability is Ethical**
No stability is valid if dignity or lived experience is compromised.

### **Clause 3 — Stability is ND‑Safe**
No stability is valid if pacing is harmful or overwhelming.

### **Clause 4 — Stability is Structural**
No stability is valid if relationships drift or modules fragment.

### **Clause 5 — Stability is Self‑Maintaining**
Auto‑update protocols must remain active.

### **Clause 6 — Stability is Reviewable**
The certificate can be suspended and restored.

### **Clause 7 — Stability is Universal**
All tiers are governed by this charter.
