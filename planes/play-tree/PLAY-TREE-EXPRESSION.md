# ✨ PLAY TREE — EXPRESSION ✨
### The Expressive Layer of the Play Tree Plane

## ❗ Play Tree Boundary Rule — No Mixing with Atropus
The Play Tree is a warm, expressive, ND-safe plane. It must never mix with
Atropus, reference Atropus, or incorporate null-plane tone, logic, or
commentary. The Play Tree and Atropus operate on entirely different tonal,
structural, and constitutional layers. They remain permanently separate.

This document defines the expressive layer of the Play Tree — the tonal,
aesthetic, and sensory qualities that shape how the tree feels, moves, and
communicates. Expression is gentle, reversible, and non-narrative. It adds
warmth without pressure and color without emotional demand.

---

# 🌳 **1. Purpose of the Expression Layer**
The Expression Layer provides:

- warmth  
- softness  
- pacing  
- tone  
- sensory gentleness  
- aesthetic coherence  

It is **not** emotional, symbolic, or psychological.  
It is **tonal**, **textural**, and **ND‑safe**.

Expression is the **surface** of the Play Tree —  
the way the tree *feels* without telling the user how *they* should feel.

---

# 🌳 **2. Expression Principles**
The expressive layer follows five principles:

### **2.1 Softness**
No sharp edges.  
No harsh contrasts.  
No sudden shifts.

### **2.2 Reversibility**
Every expressive change can be undone.

### **2.3 Non‑Coercion**
Expression never pushes mood, emotion, or interpretation.

### **2.4 Warmth**
The Play Tree is a warm plane — gentle, permissive, and calm.

### **2.5 Non‑Narrative**
Expression adds tone, not story.

---

# 🌳 **3. Expressive Elements**
The Play Tree uses five expressive elements:

1. **Color Tone**  
2. **Motion**  
3. **Density**  
4. **Softness**  
5. **Pacing**  

Each element is adjustable, reversible, and non‑directive.

---

## **3.1 Color Tone**
Color tone represents:

- warmth  
- coolness  
- brightness  
- dimness  

**Rules:**

- No neon  
- No harsh red  
- No flashing  
- No symbolic color meanings  

Color tone is purely **aesthetic**, not emotional.

---

## **3.2 Motion**
Motion represents:

- gentle sway  
- soft drift  
- slow pulsing  

**Rules:**

- No sudden movement  
- No jitter  
- No attention‑demanding animation  

Motion should feel like a calm breeze.

---

## **3.3 Density**
Density represents:

- how many leaves are visible  
- how clustered branches appear  

**Rules:**

- Density must remain within safe bounds  
- Density changes must be gradual  
- Density must not imply “too much” or “not enough”  

Density is a **comfort dial**, not a performance metric.

---

## **3.4 Softness**
Softness represents:

- edge blur  
- gradient smoothness  
- line weight  

**Rules:**

- No sharp edges  
- No high‑contrast outlines  
- No aggressive shapes  

Softness keeps the Play Tree gentle.

---

## **3.5 Pacing**
Pacing represents:

- how quickly the tree responds  
- how slowly transitions occur  

**Rules:**

- Pacing must be slow by default  
- Pacing must never accelerate without user choice  
- Pacing must always be reversible  

Pacing is the **tempo** of the Play Tree.

---

# 🌳 **4. Bob Ross Microcopy Layer**
The expressive layer uses Bob‑Ross‑style microcopy to maintain warmth.

Examples:

- “Let’s give this branch a little room to breathe.”  
- “Maybe this leaf wants to drift a little slower today.”  
- “Your tree is growing beautifully — just the way it wants to.”  
- “There are no mistakes here. Only gentle adjustments.”  

Microcopy must be:

- soft  
- permissive  
- non‑directive  
- non‑evaluative  

It adds **tone**, not **meaning**.

---

# 🌳 **5. Expression Across Layers**

### **Root Expression**

### **Branch Expression**
- subtle color shifts  
- gentle motion

### **Leaf Expression**

### **Sap Flow Expression**

### **Weather Expression**
- soft overlays

---

# 🌳 **6. Safety**
The expressive layer must:

- never imply emotion  
- never demand interpretation  
- never escalate sensory load  
- always allow reset  
- always allow exit  

Expression is **supportive**, not directive.
