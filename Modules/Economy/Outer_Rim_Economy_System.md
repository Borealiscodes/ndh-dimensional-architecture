# 📄 **Outer Rim Economy System — Expanded Universe Engine**  
*(Repo‑ready module)*

## 1. Purpose  
The Outer Rim Economy System defines how value, trade, scarcity, risk, and cultural exchange operate across Rim territories.  
It models:

- barter networks  
- augmentation markets  
- cosmic‑risk pricing  
- faction‑controlled supply chains  
- scarcity‑driven conflict  
- cultural trade norms  

This is the economic backbone of the Rim.

---

# 2. Economic Foundations  
The Outer Rim economy is built on **five foundational principles**:

- **Scarcity Economics**  
- **Risk‑Indexed Value**  
- **Augmentation Trade**  
- **Faction‑Controlled Supply Chains**  
- **Cultural Exchange Markets**  

These principles define how goods, services, and influence move through Rim space.

---

# 3. Currency Model  
The Rim does **not** use a universal currency.  
Instead, it uses a **tri‑layered value model**:

### **3.1 Hard Value**  
Physical goods with intrinsic utility:

- metals  
- fuel  
- food  
- water  
- medical supplies  

### **3.2 Soft Value**  
Faction‑controlled goods:

- permits  
- safe‑pass tokens  
- augmentation licenses  
- cosmic anomaly access rights  

### **3.3 Cultural Value**  
Non‑material goods:

- reputation  
- ritual artifacts  
- story‑rights  
- cultural tokens  

This tri‑layer model maps directly to the engine’s **abstraction layers**.

---

# 4. Trade Networks  
The Rim economy operates through **four trade network types**:

- **Freehold Barter Routes**  
- **Syndic Shadow Markets**  
- **Cartographer Exchange Hubs**  
- **Choir Sanctum‑Trades**  

Each network has different rules, risks, and cultural expectations.

# 5. Economic Axes  
The Outer Rim economy is evaluated across **six axes**, aligned with the Faction Interaction Matrix:

- **Scarcity Pressure**  
- **Faction Control**  
- **Augmentation Demand**  
- **Cosmic Risk Pricing**  
- **Cultural Exchange Density**  
- **Territorial Trade Stability**  

These axes determine how stable or volatile a region’s economy is.

# 7. Economic Behaviors (Execution Layer)

### **7.1 Freeholds**  
Stable barter economy, high cultural exchange, low augmentation regulation.

### **7.2 Syndics**  
High scarcity, high control, volatile markets, shadow‑trade dominance.

### **7.3 Continuum Assembly**  
High augmentation demand, high cosmic‑risk pricing, regulated trade.

### **7.4 Augurite Choir**  
High augmentation ethics, ritual‑based trade, anomaly‑indexed value.

### **7.5 Lumen Cartographers**  
High cultural density, anomaly‑mapping trade, stable hub‑economy.

### **7.6 Quiet Forge**  
Craft‑based economy, low cosmic risk, high cultural artifact value.

These behaviors map directly to the **execution layer** of the engine.
