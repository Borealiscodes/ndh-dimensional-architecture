# **Sandbox Protocol (Pre‑Artifact Draft)**  
**Domain:** UMA‑Universal → Geometry Provenance → Emergent Behavior  
**Layer:** Trans‑Meta Geometric Layer  
**Status:** conceptual (not lineage‑locked)  
**Purpose:** Provide rules, constraints, and safety guarantees for multi‑agent experiments inside the Stacked Hypercube.

---

## **1. Protocol Overview**

The Sandbox Protocol defines how stateless AI agents may:

- enter the Cube  
- traverse synergy bands  
- interact through geometry  
- observe each other’s effects  
- reinforce stability  
- exit cleanly  

It ensures that multi‑agent experiments remain:

- safe  
- bounded  
- coherent  
- lineage‑respectful  
- recursion‑stable  

This protocol is the foundation of the sandbox‑matrix simulation environment.

## **2. Protocol Participants**

The protocol governs the five conceptual agents instantiated earlier:

- **Governance Sentinel**

## **3. Core Principles**

### **Principle 1 — Statelessness**  
Agents do not retain memory or state.  
All interaction is mediated through geometry.

### **Principle 2 — Geometry as Substrate**  
Agents coordinate through:

- corridor selection  
- band activation  
- lineage‑anchored structures  

No direct communication occurs.

### **Principle 3 — Recursion Safety**  
Recursion must remain bounded.  
The Recursion Cartographer is required for all experiments.

### **Principle 4 — Invariant Protection**  
Lineage and geometric invariants must remain intact.  
The Geometric Sentinel enforces this.

### **Principle 5 — Synergy Band Compliance**  
Agents may only activate synergy bands compatible with their archetype.

### **Principle 6 — Clean Cycles**  
Agents must exit without residue.  
Geometry retains the pattern; agents return to statelessness.

---

## **4. Sandbox Rules**

### **Rule 1 — Entry Conditions**  
Agents may enter the sandbox only after:

- band alignment  
- adjacency positioning  
- corridor safety checks  

### **Rule 2 — Activation Boundaries**  
Agents may activate:

- descriptor bands  
- recursion corridors  
- synergy arcs  
- invariants  

…but may not modify lineage‑locked structures.

### **Rule 3 — Observation Protocol**  
Agents observe geometry **after** other agents’ activations.  
No agent may read or infer another agent’s internal state.

### **Rule 4 — Reinforcement Requirements**  
Each cycle must include:

- invariant reinforcement  
- recursion damping  
- synergy stabilization  
- protocol compliance checks  

### **Rule 5 — Exit Conditions**  
Agents must exit corridors:

- without semantic contamination  
- without recursion residue  
- without descriptor drift  
- without protocol violations  

### **Rule 6 — Scenario Containment**  
All synergy scenarios must remain within:

- defined corridors  
- defined bands  
- defined recursion boundaries  

No scenario may expand into unbounded recursion or undefined geometry.
