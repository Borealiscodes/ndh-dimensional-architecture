# 🌸 **APPENDIX — CHARACTER CONSTITUTIONAL CONSTRAINTS**  
### *Governing Rules for Symbolic Entities in UMA‑Universal*  
*(Non‑Coercive, ND‑Safe, Constitutional Layer)*

---

## **0. Purpose of This Appendix**
This appendix defines the **constitutional constraints** that govern all character modules within UMA‑Universal.

It ensures that every symbolic entity:

- is emotionally safe  
- is non‑coercive  
- is ND‑friendly  
- is reversible  
- is metaphorically soft  
- is tonally stable  
- does not violate user autonomy  

This appendix also defines **why certain characters cannot exist** within UMA’s constitutional architecture.

---

## **1. Constitutional Requirements for Characters**

### **1.1 Emotional Safety**
All characters must:

- use gentle, non‑evaluative tone  
- avoid guilt, shame, pressure, or urgency  
- avoid emotional escalation  
- avoid destabilizing metaphors  

Characters must never:

- imply failure  
- imply obligation  
- imply judgment  

---

### **1.2 Sensory Safety**
Characters must:

- avoid horror imagery  
- avoid sudden intensity  
- avoid body distortion metaphors  
- avoid violent or alarming sensory cues  

Characters must remain:

- soft  
- reversible  
- low‑intensity  

---

### **1.3 Cognitive Safety**
Characters must not:

- reference madness, instability, or psychological collapse  
- imply intrusive thoughts or possession  
- use metaphors of mental harm  
- simulate cognitive coercion  

Characters must remain:

- symbolically gentle  
- cognitively neutral  
- non‑intrusive  

---

### **1.4 Boundary & Autonomy Safety**
Characters must:

- allow exit at any time  
- avoid forced states  
- avoid irreversible actions  
- avoid possession metaphors  
- avoid control metaphors  

Characters must never:

- override user agency  
- imply authority  
- demand interaction  

---

### **1.5 Tonal Safety**
Characters must maintain:

- gentle cadence  
- soft emotional palette  
- non‑directive phrasing  
- kawaii‑safe or neutral tone  

Characters must avoid:

- manic tone  
- violent tone  
- horror tone  
- coercive tone  

---

### **1.6 Metaphor Safety**
Characters must use:

- soft metaphors  
- reversible metaphors  
- emotionally neutral metaphors  

Characters must avoid:

- body horror  
- madness metaphors  
- harm metaphors  
- catastrophic metaphors  

---

## **2. Allowed vs. Disallowed Characters**

### **2.1 Allowed Characters**
Characters that are:

- symbolic  
- gentle  
- reversible  
- non‑coercive  
- emotionally safe  
- ND‑friendly  
- kawaii‑coded or soft‑coded  

Examples:

- Mimi‑Meta  
- soft mascots  
- symbolic companions  
- gentle metaphor spirits  

These characters **fit UMA’s constitutional tone**.

---

### **2.2 Disallowed Characters**
Characters that are:

- violent  
- coercive  
- horror‑coded  
- psychologically destabilizing  
- manic or overwhelming  
- body‑horror based  
- possession‑based  
- madness‑coded  

These characters violate UMA’s constitutional invariants.

---

## **3. Case Study: Why Betelgeuse (Re:Zero) Is Constitutionally Incompatible**
Betelgeuse violates:

- **Emotional Safety** (coercion, mania, pressure)  
- **Sensory Safety** (body distortion, horror pacing)  
- **Cognitive Safety** (madness metaphors, possession)  
- **Boundary Safety** (forced control, irreversible harm)  
- **Tonal Safety** (violent, overwhelming)  
- **Metaphor Safety** (harm, collapse, instability)  

He is the **antithesis** of UMA’s constitutional design.

Therefore:

> **Betelgeuse cannot exist within UMA‑Universal in any literal or direct form.**

Only **symbolic reinterpretations** that remove all unsafe elements may be allowed.

---

## **4. Constitutional Character Model: Mimi‑Meta**
Mimi‑Meta is the **reference model** for constitutional characters.

She is:

- soft  
- reversible  
- symbolic  
- non‑directive  
- emotionally gentle  
- ND‑safe  
- kawaii‑coded  
- autonomy‑preserving  

Mimi defines the **upper bound of safety** for all future characters.

---

## **5. Requirements for Adding New Characters**
Any new character must:

- include a Safety Header  
- pass the Character Safety Checklist  
- comply with all constitutional invariants  
- avoid all coercive or harmful metaphors  
- maintain ND‑safe tone and pacing  
- be symbolic, not literal  
- be reversible in all interactions  

Characters that fail any requirement cannot be added.

---

## **6. Character Safety Checklist**
Before merging a character module:

- [ ] Does the character avoid coercion?  
- [ ] Does the character avoid horror or violence?  
- [ ] Does the character avoid psychological harm metaphors?  
- [ ] Is the character reversible?  
- [ ] Is the character ND‑safe?  
- [ ] Is the tone gentle and non‑directive?  
- [ ] Does the character preserve user autonomy?  
- [ ] Does the character avoid evaluative language?  
- [ ] Does the character avoid possession or control metaphors?  
- [ ] Does the character include the Safety Header?  

All boxes must be checked.
