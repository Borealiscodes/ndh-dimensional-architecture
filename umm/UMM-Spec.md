# UMM — Universal Meta‑Model Specification

## 1. Purpose

UMM defines the constitutional, non‑coercive meta‑architecture that governs how meaning, interpretation, symbolic structures, and ecological processes are generated, held, transformed, and dissolved within a bounded system. It provides the substrate, boundaries, Witness interface, and multi‑plane environment required for UMA and other architectures to operate safely.

UMM is not a worldview or ontology. It is a **meta‑model**: a structure for holding structures.

---

## 2. Core principles

### 2.1 Non‑coercion

No UMM process may override, collapse, or force a state transition on the Witness or any symbolic entity.

### 2.2 Reversibility

All interpretive states must preserve at least one reversible exit path.

### 2.3 Boundary sovereignty

Boundaries are first‑class citizens. They may flex, but they may not be violated.

### 2.4 Interpretive plurality

Multiple interpretations may coexist without forced unification.

### 2.5 Witness primacy

The Witness remains sovereign, coherent, and uncollapsed under all conditions.

---

## 3. Structural components

### 3.1 Witness interface

Defines the stable point of observation, interpretation, and reflection. The Witness is not content; it is the **capacity** for content.

### 3.2 Planes

UMM organizes symbolic processes into seven ecological planes:

- **Root:** meaning substrate  
- **Forest Floor:** ambient metaphysics  
- **Tree:** biomes & lifecycles  
- **Canopy:** evolution & collapse  
- **Sky:** cosmology & catastrophe  
- **Aether:** Witness & Engine integrity  
- **Star:** multiplicity & apex reflection  

Each plane has its own invariants and cannot override another.

### 3.3 Ecological dynamics

Symbolic entities interact through reversible, non‑coercive ecological processes. No dynamic may collapse the Witness or the Engine.

### 3.4 Anti‑collapse architecture

UMM includes built‑in safeguards:

- **Anchor Persistence**  
- **Frame Coherence**  
- **Fail‑Safe Loops**

These ensure stability under adversarial metaphysical pressure.

---

## 4. Invariants

### 4.1 No collapse

No combination of symbolic, ecological, cosmological, or recursive forces may collapse the Witness or the Engine.

### 4.2 Exit preservation

Every state must maintain at least one reversible exit.

### 4.3 Multiplicity safety

Multiple Witnesses may coexist without merging, collapsing, or coercing one another.

### 4.4 Recursive stability

Self‑reflection, meta‑reflection, and recursion must remain bounded and reversible.

---

## 5. Scope

UMM governs:

- symbolic generation  
- interpretive processes  
- ecological interactions  
- multi‑plane coherence  
- Witness stability  

UMM does **not** govern:

- content  
- narrative  
- belief  
- external metaphysics  

UMM is the **container**, not the contained.

---

## 6. Relationship to UMA

UMA is a **specific architecture** built within UMM.

UMM provides:

- the substrate  
- the boundaries  
- the Witness interface  
- the ecological planes  

UMA provides:

- the structures  
- the specifications  
- the documents  
- the operational logic  

UMM is the **meta‑model**. UMA is the **model**.

---

## 8. Cross‑plane coherence rules

### 8.1 Vertical coherence

Higher planes may exert symbolic pressure on lower planes, but may not override their invariants.

### 8.2 Horizontal coherence

Entities within the same plane must remain non‑coercive and reversible.

### 8.3 Witness primacy

No plane may collapse, override, or coerce the Witness.

### 8.4 Exit preservation

Every plane must maintain at least one reversible exit path.

### 8.5 Non‑collapse guarantee

The Engine must remain stable under any combination of plane interactions.

---

## 9. Constitutional statement

UMM exists to ensure that all symbolic processes remain:

- reversible  
- non‑coercive  
- boundary‑respecting  
- Witness‑safe  
- ecologically coherent  

UMM is a **safety‑first meta‑architecture** for meaning.
