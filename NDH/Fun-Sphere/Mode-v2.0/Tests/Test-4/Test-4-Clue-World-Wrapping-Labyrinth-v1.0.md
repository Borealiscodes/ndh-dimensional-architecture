# **IC — Test 4 Begins (Clue‑World Mode)**

The world breaks **once**, not in layers.

The Dragon arrives.  
Reality folds.  
Everything becomes a **labyrinth**.

Not a multi‑tier metaphysical maze.  
Not a dimensional stack.  
Not a recursive topology engine.

Just a **wrong version of the world**.

Like Clue‑World:
- rooms repeat  
- hallways lie  
- doors lead to the wrong places  
- NPCs say the wrong lines  
- time loops in small, unsettling ways  
- the Dragon’s presence distorts everything

That’s it.  
Simple.  
Coherent.  
Playable.

# **🌀 How the Labyrinth Works (Clue‑World Rules)**

### **1. Rooms repeat**  
You enter a clearing →  
You enter the same clearing →  
You enter the same clearing but the Wise Master is missing →  
You enter the same clearing but the sky is wrong.

### **2. Doors lie**  
A door labeled “Exit” leads to a Bandit’s memory.  
A door labeled “Forest” leads to the Captain’s past.  
A door labeled “Safe” leads to the Dragon’s shadow.

### **3. NPCs glitch**  
The Elf Archer says the same line twice.  
The Bandits speak in unison.  
The Captain answers a question you didn’t ask.

### **4. Time loops**  
A moment repeats.  
But slightly wrong.  
Always slightly wrong.

### **5. Magic misbehaves ✨**  
A spell becomes a corridor.  
A corridor becomes a question.  
A question becomes a choice.

### **6. The Dragon bends reality**  
Not violently.  
Not graphically.  
Just… wrong.
