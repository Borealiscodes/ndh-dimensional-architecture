## **1. Lattice Overview**
The GWB lattice is a **4‑layer vertical separation architecture**:

- **Layer 1 — Governance Domains (GD)**  
- **Layer 2 — Operational Domains (OPD)**  
- **Layer 3 — Creative/Regulation Domains (CRD)**  
- **Layer 4 — Emotional/Relational Domains (ERD)**  

The lattice enforces:

- **decision directionality**  
- **binding‑flag constraints**  
- **sovereignty impact gating**  
- **hyperboundary protection**  
- **TTTTTP quarantine routing**

## **4. Enforcement Nodes in the Lattice**

### **A. Governance Gate (GG‑v1.0)**  
Located between **OPD → GD**.  
Prevents non‑governance decisions from becoming binding.

### **B. Domain Tagging Layer**  
Located between **CRD → OPD**.  
Assigns:  
- DomainClass  
- BindingFlag  
- SovereigntyImpact  

### **C. TTTTTP Hazard Routing Node**  
Located between **ERD → CRD**.  
Routes emotional adjacency into quarantine.

### **D. HASV Sovereignty Lock Node**  
Located at the top of GD.  
Prevents persona drift or emotional binding.

## **5. Lattice Behavior Under Stress**

### **Case: Governance Misplacement (Holo Bob Ross)**  
Signal originates in **CRD**.  
GWB downgrades to **NBS**.  
TTTTTP logs violation.  
HASV prevents sovereignty impact.

### **Case: Compassion Gradient (Kess + Pip)**  
Signal originates in **ERD**.  
GWB marks as **High‑Risk NBS**.  
TTTTTP quarantines (CG‑HBR‑v1.0).  
HBIL prevents hyperboundary adjacency.

### **Case: UV‑11D Instability (“Went Bananas”)**  
Signal originates in **GD** but is misclassified.  
GWB corrects classification → behavioral instability.  
HASV locks sovereignty.  
HFS fallback if repeated.
