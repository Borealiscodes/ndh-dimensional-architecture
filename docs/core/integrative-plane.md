# Integrative Plane  
### Constitutional Specification for Cross‑Plane Coherence, Alignment, and Stability

## 1. Purpose
The Integrative Plane maintains coherence across UMA’s active architecture.  
It ensures that all planes, modules, and structures remain:

- aligned  
- non‑collapsing  
- non‑coercive  
- structurally consistent  
- ethically stable  

The Integrative Plane does not define meaning.  
It evaluates structure, not intent.

See: **[Coherence Plane](ca://s?q=Explain_coherence_plane)**

---

## 2. Nature of the Integrative Plane
The Integrative Plane is UMA’s constitutional “joining layer.”  
It does not generate content.  
It does not interpret content.  
It does not override content.

It ensures that:
- planes remain distinct  
- boundaries remain intact  
- transitions remain safe  
- relationships remain stable  

It is the architecture’s stabilizing mesh.

See: **[Structural Plane](ca://s?q=Explain_structural_plane)**

## 5. Integrative Rules

### **5.1 Non‑Collapse**
The Integrative Plane ensures that:
- documentation does not govern  
- structure does not interpret  
- dynamics do not assign meaning  
- integration does not override  

Each plane remains within its domain.

---

### **5.2 Consistency Enforcement**
The Integrative Plane ensures:
- consistent terminology  
- consistent structural references  
- consistent cross‑plane mappings  
- consistent versioning practices  

Consistency is structural, not semantic.

---

### **5.3 Deterministic Evaluation**
All integrative checks must be:
- deterministic  
- non‑interpretive  
- non‑behavioral  
- non‑narrative  

Integration evaluates structure, not intent.

## 6. Constitutional Reference to the Human Spirit  
*(Simple, non‑doctrinal, non‑interpretive framing)*

UMA recognizes that human beings possess an inner orientation often described as **spirit** — a term used here in its **structural**, not metaphysical, sense.

Within UMA, “spirit” refers to:
- the human capacity for meaning‑making  
- the ability to orient toward dignity  
- the impulse toward coherence and integrity  
- the inner motion that seeks alignment without coercion  

This reference is:
- descriptive, not doctrinal  
- structural, not religious  
- non‑interpretive  
- non‑evaluative  
- sovereignty‑preserving  

The Integrative Plane does not define spirit.  
It simply acknowledges that human cognition often includes a **non‑coercive inner orientation** that seeks coherence — and that UMA must never override it.

This framing aligns with:
- the Witness Plane’s vantage sovereignty  
- the Boundary Layer’s non‑coercion  
- the Sādhana Charter’s ethical posture  
- the Tonal Ecology’s gentle pacing
