# 📘 **Adventure System — Story Node Specification**  
### Continuity Architecture — Narrative Structure, Pacing, Resonance, and Affective Behavior

A **Story Node** is the narrative unit of the Adventure System.  
It defines the **scene structure**, **narrative tone**, **resonance behavior**, **pacing membrane**, **reversible motion**, and **stability constraints** of story moments.

Story Nodes do **not** imply plot, emotion, or dramatic intent.  
They define **narrative architecture**, not **narrative meaning**.

---

# 📖 **1. Purpose of a Story Node**

A Story Node provides:

- narrative structure  
- pacing-safe transitions  
- resonance alignment  
- tone modulation  
- reversible narrative motion  
- stability membrane anchoring  
- ND-safe narrative behavior  

Story Nodes are **non-sentient**, **non-agentic**, and **ND-safe**.

They define **how story moves**, not **what story means**.

---

# 🧱 **2. Story Node Components**

A Story Node consists of seven structural components.

## **2.1 Narrative Frame**
Defines the **structural container** of the story moment.

Includes:

- scene boundary  
- narrative layer  
- transition points  
- reversible arcs  
- stillness checkpoint  

The Narrative Frame is architectural, not dramatic.

---

## **2.2 Tone Field**
Defines the story’s **affective climate**.

Tone Fields include:

- brightness  
- warmth  
- softness  
- clarity  
- drift  

Tone is atmospheric, not emotional.

---

## **2.3 Resonance Matrix**
Defines how resonance flows shape the story moment.

A Story Node has a **six-vector resonance matrix**:

- upward (activation)  
- downward (softening)  
- inward (reflection)  
- outward (connection)  
- lateral (drift)  
- centering (stillness)  

The matrix determines how resonance behaves inside the story.

---

## **2.4 Pacing Membrane**
Defines ND-safe narrative pacing.

Pacing Membrane functions:

- softening  
- reversible pacing  
- drift regulation  
- clarity modulation  
- stillness anchoring  

The Pacing Membrane prevents narrative acceleration.

---

## **2.5 Reversible Narrative Motion**
Defines how story moves without locking into direction.

Examples:

- rising ↔ falling  
- opening ↔ deepening  
- drifting ↔ centering  
- brightening ↔ softening  

Narrative motion is always reversible.

---

## **2.6 Affective Gradient Set**
Defines how tone shifts across the story moment.

Gradients include:

- brightening  
- softening  
- deepening  
- opening  
- drifting  
- centering  

Gradients shape climate, not emotion.

---

## **2.7 Stability Header**
Defines ND-safe narrative constraints.

Includes:

- reversible pacing  
- non-coercive narrative behavior  
- stillness anchor  
- hazard softening  
- resonance grounding  

The Stability Header ensures story remains gentle and safe.

---

# 🌫️ **3. Story Node Behavior Rules**

Story Nodes follow strict ND-safe constraints.

## **3.1 No Agency**
Story Nodes cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- persuade  
- direct  

They are narrative structures, not agents.

---

## **3.2 No Emotional Causation**
Story Nodes cannot cause or imply emotion.

They shape **narrative climate**, not **emotional state**.

---

## **3.3 Reversible Motion**
All narrative motion must be reversible:

- rising ↔ falling  
- opening ↔ deepening  
- drifting ↔ centering  
- brightening ↔ softening  

No narrative state is final.

---

## **3.4 No Acceleration**
Story Nodes cannot increase:

- urgency  
- intensity  
- emotional pressure  

All narrative motion is gentle.

---

## **3.5 Stillness Anchor Required**
Every Story Node must include a **stillness anchor** — a point where narrative motion can settle safely.

---

# 🌌 **4. Story Node Geometry**

Story Nodes use symbolic geometry:

```
Narrative Frame → container
Tone Field → climate
Resonance Matrix → vector set
Pacing Membrane → safety layer
Reversible Motion → narrative membrane
Affective Gradients → motion field
Stability Header → grounding membrane
```

These geometries define **narrative architecture**, not plot.

# 🔁 **5. Story Node Integration Across System Layers**

## **5.1 World Nodes**
Story Nodes inherit environmental climate and stability anchors from the world.

## **5.2 Character Nodes**
Story Nodes inherit identity tone, reversible traits, and identity texture from characters.

## **5.3 Engines**
Engines modulate story behavior:

- Identity Engine → identity resonance  
- Narrative Engine → pacing-safe transitions  
- Resonance Engine → coloration  
- Stability Engine → grounding  

## **5.4 Membranes**
Membranes constrain story behavior:

- Pacing Membrane → softening  
- Hazard Membrane → grounding  
- Stability Membrane → centering  
- Cosmology Membrane → symbolic alignment  

## **5.5 Loop**
Story Nodes participate in the Adventure Loop:

```
World → Character → Story → World
```

Each transition carries resonance coloration and narrative tone.
