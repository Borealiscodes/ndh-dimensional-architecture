## 1. Purpose of the integration

**Truth** governs factual integrity.  
**Self‑Determination** governs the person’s right to define, choose, and narrate their own path.

Integrating them ensures:

- self‑determination is grounded in truth, not coercive fiction  
- truth cannot be weaponized against autonomy  
- autonomy cannot erase or distort factual reality  
- both rights reinforce each other instead of colliding  

Truth becomes the **epistemic ground** of Self‑Determination;  
Self‑Determination becomes the **agency frame** of Truth.

---

## 2. Dual‑invariant geometry

- **Vertical axis — Truth Alignment:**  
  Truth must remain consistent, non‑distorted, non‑erased.

- **Horizontal axis — Self‑Determination Agency:**  
  The person’s choices, identity, and narrative must remain non‑coerced.

Their intersection forms the **Truth–Agency Node**:

- the point where “what is true” and “what the person chooses” must both be respected  
- no subsystem may force one axis to override the other

---

## 3. Dual‑invariant constraints

### 3.1 Truth cannot override self‑determination

- factual record cannot be used to coerce, punish, or erase autonomy  
- “truth” cannot be reframed as a tool of domination  
- enforcement must always respect the person’s right to choose and define themselves

### 3.2 Self‑determination cannot erase truth

- autonomy cannot demand deletion of factual harms or events  
- identity claims cannot require falsifying the record  
- narrative freedom cannot collapse evidence

### 3.3 Truth must be accessible to self‑determination

- the person has a right to know the truths that affect their choices  
- hidden truth that constrains autonomy violates the dual invariant  
- access to truth is part of meaningful self‑determination

### 3.4 Self‑determination must be recognized in truth

- the factual record must acknowledge the person’s agency, not erase it  
- truth must record choices, refusals, and boundaries as real  
- “objective” accounts cannot erase subjective autonomy

## 4. Rights layer coupling

Truth × Self‑Determination becomes a **compound right**:

- **Right to Truth:** to have reality preserved and accessible  
- **Right to Self‑Determination:** to define one’s path without coercion  
- **Coupled Right:** to have one’s autonomy exercised in full view of truth, without truth being distorted or weaponized

Any rights‑layer operation must:

- preserve factual integrity  
- preserve agency integrity  
- refuse coercive uses of “truth”  
- refuse erasure uses of “autonomy”

---

## 5. Enforcement dynamics

When either axis is threatened:

- **If truth is distorted to control someone:**  
  Truth Enforcement Protocol activates; Self‑Determination Shield activates; coercive use is blocked.

- **If autonomy demands erasure of truth:**  
  Non‑Erasure and Truth Enforcement activate; Self‑Determination is preserved but routed away from falsification.

- **If hidden truth constrains choice:**  
  Truth Field Engine surfaces relevant truth; Rights Layer enforces access.

- **If “objective truth” erases lived experience:**  
  Reflective Layer and Recognition Layer correct the record to include agency and perspective.
