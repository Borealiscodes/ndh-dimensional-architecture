# 🗄️ **UNTRUSTED ARCHIVE**

## **Purpose**  
Provide a safe, isolated location for concepts, tiles, workflows, or ideas flagged as untrusted by the BS Detection Kernel or Safety Interrupt.  
Prevent contamination of the main architecture while preserving flagged content for later review, refinement, or deletion.

---

## **What Belongs Here**  
Items are placed in the Untrusted Archive when they trigger:

- **Practical BS flags** — unclear, undoable, vague  
- **Conceptual BS flags** — contradictory, inflated, incoherent  
- **Emotional BS flags** — pressured, ego‑driven, destabilizing  
- **Ethical BS flags** — unjust, erasing lived experience, harmful  
- **Structural BS flags** — drift, duplication, misplacement  

These items are *not deleted* — they are quarantined.

---

## **Archive Workflow**

1. **Receive Flagged Item**  
   From BS Kernel or Safety Interrupt.

2. **Label**  
   Mark with one or more tags:  
   - `practical-bs`  
   - `conceptual-bs`  
   - `emotional-bs`  
   - `ethical-bs`  
   - `structural-bs`

3. **Store**  
   Place the item in this file or in a subfolder:  
   ```
   /philosophy-couch/untrusted/
   ```

4. **Review (Optional)**  
   When stable, revisit items to:  
   - refine  
   - relocate  
   - rewrite  
   - or permanently retire

5. **Protect**  
   Items in this archive do not influence active layers, tiles, or workflows.

---

## **Archive Structure**

### **1. Items**  
A list of flagged concepts, modules, or fragments.

### **2. Tags**  
Each item includes BS tags for clarity.

### **3. Notes**  
Optional context explaining why the item was flagged.

### **4. Status**  
- `pending-review`  
- `refined`  
- `retired`  

---

## **Example Entry**

```
## Item: “Overclocked Workflow Draft”
Tags: structural-bs, practical-bs
Status: pending-review
Notes: Too many steps, unclear purpose, violates ND pacing.
```

---

## **In One Line**  
The Untrusted Archive is the system’s quarantine zone — storing flagged BS safely away from the architecture while preserving it for later review.
