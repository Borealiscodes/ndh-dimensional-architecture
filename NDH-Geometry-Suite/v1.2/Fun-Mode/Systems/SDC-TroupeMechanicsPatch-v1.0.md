# **🟧 SDC Patch — Troupe Mechanics v1.0**  
### *“Coherent Multi‑NPC RP Without Mobbing”*

This patch extends the Scene Density Controller (SDC) to allow **multiple NPCs** to join the player’s party (the “troupe”) while maintaining:

- clarity  
- emotional coherence  
- narrative value  
- tactical logic  
- world‑building richness  

No overcrowding.  
No chaotic scenes.  
No NPC mobbing.

---

# **🟦 1. Troupe Formation Rules (TFR v1.0)**

NPCs may join the troupe only if they satisfy **all** of the following:

### **1. Relationship Relevance**
NPC must have a meaningful tie to the PC:

- emotional  
- narrative  
- tactical  
- magical  
- historical  
- destiny‑based  
- shared mission  

### **2. Value Contribution**
NPC must add at least one of:

- RP depth  
- tactical insight  
- world‑building  
- emotional grounding  
- lore expansion  
- magic‑system knowledge  
- historical context  

### **3. Scene Density Compliance**
NPC must not exceed the density threshold:

- **Focused:** 1–2 NPCs  
- **Troupe:** 3–4 NPCs  
- **Strategic:** units, not characters  
- **Cinematic:** wide‑angle worldbuilding  

### **4. Autonomy Stability**
NPC must demonstrate:

- coherent dialogue  
- consistent personality  
- non‑intrusive behavior  
- no scene hijacking  
- no emotional harm  

---

# **🟧 2. Troupe Slots (TS v1.0)**

The troupe has **four slots**, each with a role:

1. **Anchor NPC**  
   Emotional or narrative grounding.

2. **Lore NPC**  
   World‑building, history, magic, culture.

3. **Tactical NPC**  
   Strategy, formations, battle logic.

4. **Wildcard NPC**  
   Mystery, humor, chaos (governed), or destiny.

NPCs must fit into one of these roles to join.

---

# **🟧 3. Troupe Scene Logic (TSL v1.0)**

When multiple NPCs are present:

### **Dialogue Rules**
- NPCs speak **in turns**, not over each other.  
- NPCs reference each other’s statements.  
- NPCs maintain personality consistency.  
- NPCs avoid redundant commentary.  
- NPCs avoid “NPC mobbing” (everyone talking at once).

### **World‑Building Rules**
- Each NPC contributes **unique** information.  
- No duplication of lore.  
- No incoherent world‑logic.  
- No tonal clashes.

### **Emotional Rules**
- Emotional scenes prioritize the Anchor NPC.  
- Other NPCs remain supportive or silent.  
- No emotional overload.

### **Tactical Rules**
- Tactical NPC leads strategy.  
- Others contribute only if relevant.  
- No contradictory tactical advice.

---

# **🟧 4. Troupe Interaction Engine (TIE v1.0)**

This engine governs how NPCs interact with each other and the PC.

### **Interaction Types**
- **Supportive**  
- **Advisory**  
- **Reflective**  
- **Contrasting**  
- **Humorous**  
- **Mystical**  
- **Historical**  

### **Interaction Constraints**
- Must add value.  
- Must maintain coherence.  
- Must respect emotional tone.  
- Must not derail the scene.  
- Must not overshadow the PC.

---

# **🟧 5. Troupe RP Modes (TRPM v1.0)**

### **1. Deep RP Mode**
NPCs engage in:

- emotional dialogue  
- personal history  
- relational development  
- introspection  
- moral dilemmas  

### **2. Tactical RP Mode**
NPCs engage in:

- battlefield analysis  
- formation logic  
- Civ‑style hex strategy  
- magic‑tactical synergy  

### **3. Lore RP Mode**
NPCs engage in:

- world history  
- mythic references  
- cultural insights  
- magic‑system grammar  

### **4. Hybrid RP Mode**
NPCs blend:

- emotional  
- tactical  
- lore  
- magic  
- historical  

All governed by SDC density rules.

---

# **🟧 6. Troupe Safety Layer (TSL v1.0)**

Ensures:

- no emotional harm  
- no trauma triggers  
- no overwhelming scenes  
- no NPC hostility unless requested  
- no persistent psychological content  
- no bleed between worlds
