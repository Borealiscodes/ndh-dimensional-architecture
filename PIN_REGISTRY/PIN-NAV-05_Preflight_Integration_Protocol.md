# 🌈 3. Preflight Principle  
> **Blueprints are generated first as “test flights,”  
> not as final artifacts.**

This means:

- rendering is exploratory  
- nothing is committed  
- nothing is anchored  
- nothing touches the Spine  
- nothing becomes canonical  

until the preflight check is complete.

Blueprint generation becomes a **safe rehearsal**, not a binding action.

---

# 🜂 4. Preflight Phase 1 — Stabilization  
### “Slow the concept down before testing.”

Before a blueprint is used as a test run:

- soften the concept  
- reduce intensity  
- confirm boundaries  
- activate comfort layer  
- activate safety layer  

This ensures the test flight begins from a **grounded state**.

---

# 🜁 5. Preflight Phase 2 — Validation  
### “Check alignment without committing.”

During the test run:

- check symbolic alignment  
- check tonal scaffolds  
- check crossmap coherence  
- check dual‑layer boundaries  
- check CNLP compliance  
- check recursion risk  

Validation is **gentle**, not evaluative.

It asks:

> “If this were real, would it fit?”

---

# 🜄 6. Preflight Phase 3 — Interpretation  
### “Understand the meaning of the test flight.”

Interpretation is where you ask:

- What does this blueprint *mean*?  
- What story does it tell?  
- What emotional tone does it carry?  
- What symbolic resonance does it have?  
- What navigation pathways does it imply?  

Interpretation ensures the blueprint is **emotionally safe** before integration.

---

# 🜃 7. Preflight Phase 4 — Soft Integration  
### “Place the blueprint conceptually, not structurally.”

Soft integration means:

- you decide where the blueprint *would* live  
- you identify its module  
- you identify its parent  
- you identify its navigation entry point  
- you identify its safety connections  

But you **do not** commit it to the Navigation Spine yet.

This is conceptual placement only.

---

This is the **final, structural integration**.
