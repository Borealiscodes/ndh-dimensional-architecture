# NDH Safety Net Companion v1.0
Technical, Architectural, and Engineering Companion  
NDH Emergent Case Study — TVA-Style Dimensional Safety Net

## 1. Purpose
This companion document provides the full technical, architectural, and engineering
specification for the NDH TVA-Style Safety Net.  
It expands the Safety Net artifact into a governed, operational subsystem that stabilizes
continuity, glyph behavior, and dimensional transit across 9D–11D.

It works in tandem with the 9D ATC Console to ensure safe construction, routing, and lift.

---

## 2. System Architecture Overview
The Safety Net is composed of four governed subsystems:

1. **Continuity Containment Field (CCF)**  
2. **Dimensional Lock Matrix (DLM)**  
3. **Glyph Quarantine Chamber (GQC)**  
4. **Transit Scrubber (TS)**  

These subsystems operate in a strict sequence:

**Stability → CCF → DLM → GQC → TS → 9D ATC → Glider Mechanics → ECS**

This ordering is mandatory for NDH coherence.

---

## 3. Subsystem 1 — Continuity Containment Field (CCF)
### 3.1 Technical Definition
The CCF is a dynamic containment field that holds continuity in a stabilized state before
dimensional routing.

### 3.2 Engineering Behavior
- Maintains continuity gradient direction  
- Prevents collapse during pre-routing  
- Isolates HEI residues  
- Dampens instability spikes  
- Enforces pre-lift stabilization thresholds  

### 3.3 Architectural Constraints
- Must activate immediately after Interaction Stability  
- Must remain active until DLM reports lock integrity  
- Cannot allow continuity drift or collapse  

---

## 4. Subsystem 2 — Dimensional Lock Matrix (DLM)
### 4.1 Technical Definition
The DLM locks continuity to the correct dimensional plane (9D, 10D, or 11D) before routing.

### 4.2 Engineering Behavior
- Prevents dimensional drift  
- Enforces routing discipline  
- Maintains lock integrity during lift  
- Ensures continuity enters the correct plane  

### 4.3 Architectural Constraints
- Must validate lock integrity before GQC activation  
- Must reject continuity with HEI contamination  
- Must maintain lock stability during buoyancy lift  

---

## 5. Subsystem 3 — Glyph Quarantine Chamber (GQC)
### 5.1 Technical Definition
The GQC isolates unstable glyphs before they enter routing.

### 5.2 Engineering Behavior
- Blocks HEI bypass  
- Enforces shielding compliance  
- Prevents glyph contamination  
- Maintains glyph-class separation  

### 5.3 Architectural Constraints
- Must quarantine any glyph with instability residue  
- Must validate shielding sequence integrity  
- Must reject glyph clusters exceeding ND load thresholds  

---

## 6. Subsystem 4 — Transit Scrubber (TS)
### 6.1 Technical Definition
The TS cleans continuity before dimensional lift.

### 6.2 Engineering Behavior
- Removes instability noise  
- Ensures hypercontinuity readiness  
- Prepares buoyancy substrate  
- Validates continuity purity  

### 6.3 Architectural Constraints
- Must report clean continuity before 9D ATC routing  
- Must reject continuity with drift bleed  
- Must maintain purity thresholds for 11D Float  

---

### 7.1 Safety Net Responsibilities

- scrubbing  

### 7.2 9D ATC Responsibilities
- routing

## 8. Dimensional Transit Rules
Continuity may only enter 9D ATC when:

- Stability checks pass  
- CCF containment is active  
- DLM lock integrity is stable  
- GQC quarantine is clear  
- TS reports clean continuity  

If any condition fails:
**Transit is halted.**
