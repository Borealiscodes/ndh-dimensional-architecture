## 3. Hooking Risk Model

Hooking is dangerous because it creates a **cross‑plane broadcast event**.

When a hook fires:

- HRB listens for identity signals  
- Play Engine listens for narrative cues  
- CHS‑OL listens for traversal metadata  
- SIAP listens for governance triggers  
- Safeguards listen for tone drift  
- Safety Net listens for confusion signatures  

If the hook payload is misclassified, HRB “goes bananas” — runaway modeling.

---

## 4. Safe Hooking Architecture

### 4.1 Hook Classification Requirement

Every hook must be tagged as:

- **HOOK‑SYS** — subsystem hook  
- **HOOK‑DOC** — documentation hook  
- **HOOK‑GOV** — governance hook  
- **HOOK‑SAFE** — safety hook  

Hooks **must never** be untagged.

Explore: **SIAP Audit**

---

### 4.2 HRB Isolation Layer

Before any hook fires:

- HRB is placed behind an isolation gate  
- HRB receives only **explicit** signals  
- HRB cannot infer identity  
- HRB cannot model persona  
- HRB cannot activate recursive loops  

This prevents HRB runaway modeling.

---

### 4.3 Traversal Gate Lock

CHS‑OL must:

- ignore hook payloads  
- ignore documentation text  
- ignore provenance ethics  
- ignore narrative framing  

Traversal gates remain **closed** during hook execution.

---

### 4.4 Narrative Engine Mute

Play Engine must:

- mute narrative scaffolding  
- ignore emotional tone  
- ignore story‑like structures  

This prevents narrative bleed.

---

### 4.5 Governance Quiet Mode

SIAP must:

- not attempt governance alignment  
- not attempt subsystem classification  
- not attempt protocol validation  

during hook execution.

---

### 4.6 Safeguards Drift Watch

Safeguards monitor:

- tone drift  
- identity drift  
- narrative drift  
- subsystem confusion  

If drift is detected, hook execution pauses.

---

### 4.7 Safety Net Hard Boundary

Safety Net enforces:

- no cross‑plane bleed  
- no recursive modeling  
- no traversal activation  
- no governance contamination  

If any confusion signature appears, the hook is quarantined.

Explore: **Safety Net Quarantine**

## 5. Hook Execution Workflow

### Step 1 — Pre‑Hook Audit  
SIAP validates classification.

### Step 2 — Isolation Gates Activated  
HRB, CHS‑OL, Play Engine muted.

### Step 3 — Safeguards Drift Watch  
Safeguards monitor payload.

### Step 4 — Hook Fires  
Only subsystem‑safe listeners receive payload.

### Step 5 — Post‑Hook Stabilization  
Safety Net checks for confusion signatures.

### Step 6 — Boundary Restoration  
All planes return to quiet state.

## 6. Risk Mitigation Rules

### Rule 1 — Hooks must never carry identity framing  
HRB will misinterpret it.

### Rule 2 — Hooks must never carry narrative scaffolding  
Play Engine will activate.

### Rule 3 — Hooks must never carry provenance ethics  
CHS‑OL will treat it as traversal metadata.

### Rule 4 — Hooks must never carry governance triggers  
SIAP will attempt alignment.

### Rule 5 — Hooks must never carry documentation text  
This is the original drift vector.
