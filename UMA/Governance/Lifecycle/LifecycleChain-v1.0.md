## **1. Purpose**
Lifecycle Chain v1.0 defines the **temporal sequencing rules** for UMA subsystems:

- when they activate  
- how they transition  
- how they deactivate  
- how they inherit state  
- how they maintain continuity  
- how they avoid drift  

It is the **temporal spine** complementing the structural spine.

---

## **2. Lifecycle Phases**

### **Phase 1 — Genesis**  
Subsystem enters existence.

- Registered in **Pin Registry**  
- Added to **Roots Ledger**  
- Assigned initial adjacency edges  
- Given a containment profile  

**Anchoring Pin:**  
**PIN‑001** — UMA Core Genesis

---

### **Phase 2 — Initialization**  
Subsystem becomes structurally recognized.

- SIAP routing established  
- Adjacency edges validated  
- HRD constraints applied  
- Stability baseline created  

**Anchoring Pins:**  
- **PIN‑002** — SIAP Spine  
- **PIN‑005** — UMM Frame

---

### **Phase 3 — Activation**  
Subsystem becomes operational.

- Procedural drivers engaged  
- Traversal permissions granted  
- Containment gates tested  
- Operational layer linked (CHS‑OL)  

**Anchoring Pin:**  
**PIN‑020** — Procedural Driver Activation

---

### **Phase 4 — Operation**  
Subsystem performs its function.

- Traversal allowed  
- Adjacency enforced  
- Containment monitored  
- Stability audited (HBR)  
- Ethical constraints enforced (HRD)  

**Anchoring Pins:**  
- **PIN‑004** — CHS‑OL  
- **PIN‑050** — Play Engine Containment  
- **PIN‑024** — Bleed‑Control Verification

---

### **Phase 5 — Extension**  
Subsystem gains new capabilities.

- New adjacency edges added  
- Lifecycle hooks expanded  
- Stability rules updated  
- Containment rules refined  

**Anchoring Pin:**  
**PIN‑081** — Lifecycle Chain Extension

---

### **Phase 6 — Integration**  
Subsystem merges with broader UMA architecture.

- Roots lineage updated  
- Registry version incremented  
- Adjacency matrix expanded  
- Traversal rules harmonized  

**Anchoring Pins:**  
- **PIN‑060** — Roots Registry  
- **PIN‑071** — Adjacency Expansion

---

### **Phase 7 — Continuity**  
Subsystem becomes part of UMA’s long‑term spine.

- Inheritance stabilized  
- Drift prevented  
- Temporal coherence maintained  
- Cross‑subsystem compatibility ensured  

**Anchoring Pin:**  
**PIN‑061** — Continuity Anchor

---

### **Phase 8 — Deactivation**  
Subsystem shuts down safely.

- Traversal revoked  
- Containment gates closed  
- Adjacency edges removed  
- Roots lineage archived  

This phase is defined but not yet pinned in v1.0.

## **4. Why Lifecycle Chain v1.0 Works Even Without Full CHS Cube Completion**

Because UMA’s spine now provides:

- structural continuity (Roots)  
- adjacency constraints (Matrix v1.1)  
- containment gates (Play Engine)  
- operational traversal (CHS‑OL)  
- stability audits (HBR)  
- ethical constraints (HRD)  

Full hypercube geometry is **not required** for lifecycle sequencing.

The spine works.
