# Core Principles  
### The Gentle Foundations of Your Project

## 1. Purpose
Core Principles define the soft, steady ideas that guide your project.  
They are not rules.  
They are not demands.  
They are simply the friendly truths your project rests on.

These principles help you stay aligned, calm, and clear as you build.

---

## 2. Principle: Clarity
Clarity keeps the project readable, understandable, and kind.  
It means choosing simple language, gentle structure, and steady pacing.

Clarity is a gift to your future self.

---

## 3. Principle: Gentleness
Gentleness means avoiding urgency, pressure, and overwhelm.  
It means building in small strokes, respecting pacing, and keeping the project light.

Gentleness is the tone of the canvas.

---

## 4. Principle: Stability
Stability means keeping the project non-collapsing and predictable.  
It means adding structure slowly, checking coherence, and stopping early.

Stability is the frame around the painting.

---

## 5. Principle: Curiosity
Curiosity keeps the project alive.  
It means exploring ideas without forcing them, trying small experiments, and enjoying discovery.

Curiosity is the spark in the brush.

---

## 6. Principle: Non-Coercion
Non-coercion means the project never demands, pressures, or forces.  
It grows at the pace you choose.  
It remains friendly, patient, and open.

Non-coercion is the air in the room.

---

## 7. Principle: Respect for Pacing
Respect for pacing means stopping when you feel tired, pausing when needed, and working only when it feels right.

Pacing is part of the architecture.

---

## 8. Principle: Small Strokes
Small strokes keep the project manageable and joyful.  
One line at a time.  
One idea at a time.  
One gentle step.

Small strokes make big beauty.

---

## 9. Summary
These Core Principles form the gentle foundation of your project.  
They keep the work calm, steady, and joyful.
