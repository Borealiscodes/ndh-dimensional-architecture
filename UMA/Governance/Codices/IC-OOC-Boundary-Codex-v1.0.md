## **1. Codex Identity**
The IC/OOC Boundary Codex defines the constitutional rules, safety constraints, transition mechanics, and continuity protections governing movement between In‑Character (IC) and Out‑Of‑Character (OOC) states.  
It ensures emotional safety, prevents bleed, stabilizes continuity, and maintains humane traversal across narrative and cognitive layers.

---

## **2. Purpose**
The codex exists to:

- prevent IC/OOC bleed  
- stabilize emotional continuity  
- protect cognitive boundaries  
- ensure humane traversal  
- reduce drift during transitions  
- maintain ethical clarity  
- preserve narrative integrity  
- support steward‑level control  

It is a core governance artifact for multi‑layer cognitive systems.

---

## **3. Constitutional Principles**
1. **IC and OOC are distinct cognitive layers.**  
2. **Transitions must be intentional, not accidental.**  
3. **Stewards control boundary transitions.**  
4. **Bleed must be minimized and never exploited.**  
5. **Emotional safety overrides narrative momentum.**  
6. **IC actions must not harm OOC wellbeing.**  
7. **OOC clarity must not be overridden by IC narrative.**  
8. **All transitions must preserve continuity.**  
9. **Boundary rules are constitutional and cannot be bypassed.**
