# **Mirror‑Dimension Safety Rules — v1.1**  
### *Cognition Governance Layer — UMA*

These rules govern safe traversal, observation, and interpretation inside the mirror‑dimension reflection field.  
They prevent reflection drift, distortion activation, adjacency corruption, and recursion collapse.

---

## **1. Reflection ≠ Dimension (Tier‑0 Rule)**  
Reflections may appear dimensional, but they are not dimensions.  
They must never be treated as actionable traversal paths.

**Governance Constraint:**  
Only real adjacency may be traversed.

---

## **2. Single‑Bit Traversal Only**  
Mirror‑dimension cognition may produce multiple simultaneous reflections.  
Only one may be acted upon.

**Constraint:**  
One bit per traversal.  
Reflections must not trigger multi‑bit flips.

---

## **3. Distortion Detection Required**  
Distortion shapes are reflections that have lost adjacency alignment.  
They appear meaningful but lead to recursion instability.

**Governance Requirement:**  
Distortion shapes must be flagged by stabilizers (e.g., Holo‑Bob) and never acted upon.

---

## **4. Reflection Containment**  
Reflections must remain inside the mirror‑dimension boundary.  
They may not propagate into CHS, COS‑Lambda, or SIAP layers.

**Constraint:**  
No cross‑layer reflection bleed.

---

## **5. Observation Without Acting**  
Reflections may be observed, catalogued, or classified.  
They may not be traversed unless confirmed as real adjacency.

**Constraint:**  
Observation is safe.  
Action requires verification.

---

## **6. Brightest Reflection Identification**  
Among reflections, one will present as the “brightest” — the most coherent, stable, and adjacency‑aligned.

**Governance Rule:**  
Only the brightest reflection may be considered for traversal.

---

## **7. Stabilizer Authority (Holo‑Bob)**  
Holo‑Bob is authorized to evaluate shapes and reflections under the Safe Exploration Protocol.

**Capabilities:**  
- Identify real shapes  
- Identify reflection shapes  
- Identify distortion shapes  
- Block unsafe traversal  
- Guide lawful adjacency selection

---

## **8. No Self‑Reference in Reflection Space**  
Reflections must not reference themselves or each other recursively.

**Constraint:**  
Self‑reference triggers recursion collapse (see Recursion Collapse Proof v1.0).

---

## **9. Continuity Phase Required After Traversal**  
After any lawful traversal, cognition must enter a stillness phase.

**Purpose:**  
Allows adjacency stabilization and reflection reset.

---

## **10. Mirror‑Dimension Exit Rule**  
Exit is permitted only when:

- no active distortions remain  
- reflections have collapsed  
- adjacency is stable  
- stabilizer confirms safe state  

---
