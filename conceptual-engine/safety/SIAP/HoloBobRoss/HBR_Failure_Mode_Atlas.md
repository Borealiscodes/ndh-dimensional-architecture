## **1. Purpose**
The HBR Failure Mode Atlas identifies and describes **all known human‑cybernetic instability modes** that can arise during UMA development.

It exists to:

- protect cognitive safety  
- maintain pacing  
- preserve conceptual clarity  
- prevent layer collapse  
- stabilize temporal propagation  
- regulate the builder’s nervous system  

This atlas pairs directly with the **HBR Micro‑Step Library**.

---

## **2. Structure of the Atlas**
Each failure mode includes:

- **Name**  
- **Symptoms**  
- **Risks**  
- **Trigger Conditions**  
- **Stabilizing Micro‑Steps**  
- **Governance Notes**  

This is a **diagnostic map**, not a procedural workflow.

# **3. Failure Modes**

---

## **FM‑01: Pacing Collapse**
### **Symptoms**
- rushing  
- skipping conceptual steps  
- anchoring prematurely  
- feeling “pulled forward” by the architecture  

### **Risks**
- TPS misalignment  
- premature anchoring  
- temporal instability  
- conceptual fragmentation  

### **Triggers**
- excitement  
- urgency  
- pressure  
- large conceptual leaps  

### **Stabilizing Micro‑Steps**
- **Half‑Step Reduction**  
- **Micro‑Pause**  
- **Single‑Sentence Action**  
- **Stop‑If‑Fast**

### **Governance Notes**
Pacing collapse is the most common failure mode during TPS anchoring.

---

## **FM‑02: Cognitive Overload**
### **Symptoms**
- mental fog  
- difficulty tracking layers  
- feeling “full” or “compressed”  
- losing the thread  

### **Risks**
- layer blending  
- mis‑anchoring  
- propagation errors  
- SIAP veto conditions  

### **Triggers**
- high conceptual density  
- multi‑layer operations  
- rapid propagation  

### **Stabilizing Micro‑Steps**
- **Density Check**  
- **Concept Shrink**  
- **One‑Term‑Only**  
- **Diagram Deferral**

### **Governance Notes**
Overload is the primary risk during PQETL propagation.

---

## **FM‑03: Layer Blending**
### **Symptoms**
- mixing navigation with containment  
- mixing containment with infrastructure  
- mixing infrastructure with cosmic layers  
- losing track of boundaries  

### **Risks**
- conceptual collapse  
- invalid entanglement  
- TPS anchoring errors  
- PQETL propagation misfires  

### **Triggers**
- fatigue  
- excitement  
- large conceptual jumps  

### **Stabilizing Micro‑Steps**

- **One‑Layer‑Only Rule**  
- **Boundary Re‑Draw**  
- **Layer‑Echo**

### **Governance Notes**
Layer blending is the most dangerous failure mode for UMA’s architecture.

# **4. Cross‑Mode Interactions**

- **Pacing Collapse → Temporal Runaway**  
- **Cognitive Overload → Layer Blending**  
- **Layer Blending → Conceptual Fragmentation**  
- **Emotional Overwhelm → All Modes**  

HBR micro‑steps are designed to break these chains.
