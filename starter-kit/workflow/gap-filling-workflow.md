# Gap-Filling Workflow  
### A Gentle, Bob-Ross-Style Method for Adding to Your Project

## 1. Purpose
This workflow helps you fill gaps in your project with calm, friendly, low-pressure steps.  
It is designed to keep your work steady, joyful, and non-collapsing.

Think of it as your “happy little gap-filling guide.”

---

## 2. Step 1 — Notice the Gap
Look softly at your project.  
Find one tiny gap — a missing definition, a boundary that needs clarity, a relationship that needs a sentence.

No urgency.  
Gaps are friendly invitations.

---

## 3. Step 2 — Identify the Region
Ask gently:

“Where does this gap live?”

Examples:
- core  
- workflow  
- meta  
- boundaries  
- operations  
- stability  
- docs  

Naming the region helps you choose the right brush.

---

## 4. Step 3 — Pick a Small Brush
Choose the smallest possible tool.

Examples:
- one definition  
- one clause  
- one boundary line  
- one relationship block  
- one gentle clarification  

Small brushes make the project light and joyful.

---

## 5. Step 4 — Add One Gentle Stroke
Write one small, clear, non-coercive line.

Examples:
- “This component may not override X.”  
- “This boundary remains non-collapsing.”  
- “This rule protects pacing.”  

One stroke.  
Not ten.  
Not a whole tree — just a happy little branch.

---

## 6. Step 5 — Step Back and Look
Pause.  
Ask softly:

“Does this feel stable?”

If yes — you’re done.  
If not — add one more tiny stroke.

Let the canvas breathe.

---

## 7. Step 6 — Stop Early
Bob Ross rule:  
Stop before you think you’re finished.

Leaving space is part of the art.  
Projects grow best when not overfilled.

---

## 8. Step 7 — Commit the Change
Save the file.  
Commit the update.  
Let the canvas dry.

No pressure.  
No urgency.  
Just a gentle commit.

---

## 9. Step 8 — Return to Stillness
Stillness is part of the workflow.  
It keeps your project gentle, stable, and non-collapsing.

Stillness is where the next idea arrives.

---

## 10. Step 9 — Choose the Next Gap
When you’re ready — not before — choose the next gap.

The loop is gentle.  
Never forced.

---

## 11. Summary
This gap-filling workflow keeps your project calm, stable, and joyful.  
It helps you grow your work one tiny stroke at a time.
