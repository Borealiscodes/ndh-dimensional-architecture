# 📄 Cultural Narrative Weave — Societal Story Logic  
*(High‑Level Cultural Module)*

## 1. Purpose  
The Cultural Narrative Weave defines **how different cultures in your universe interpret, respond to, and encode events**—especially cosmic, factional, and identity‑related ones.  
It governs:

- cultural myth‑making  
- ritual responses to cosmic phenomena  
- factional propaganda and public narratives  
- identity and augmentation norms  
- how stories are remembered, distorted, or erased over time  

It sits between **Cosmology / Cosmic Interaction** and **faction/quest/story systems**, acting as the “cultural lens” on everything that happens.

---

## 2. Core abstractions  

### **2.1 Cultural Thread**  
A Cultural Thread is a single interpretive strand, such as:

- myth (“the Void is a trial”)  
- doctrine (“augmentation must be anchored”)  
- taboo (“never cross a resonance fold at dusk”)  
- ritual (“we sing to dampen jitter”)  

Each thread has:

- origin (era, event, faction)  
- scope (local, regional, interstellar)  
- rigidity (how strongly enforced)  
- adaptability (how easily it changes)

### **2.2 Weave Pattern**  
A Weave Pattern is the composite of many threads within a culture:

- dominant myths  
- accepted norms  
- contested narratives  
- suppressed stories  

Patterns determine how a culture reacts to new events.

### **2.3 Cultural Lens**  
The Cultural Lens is how a culture *perceives*:

- cosmic events  
- faction actions  
- identity changes  
- economic shifts  
- combat and conflict  

---

## 3. Cultural schema  

Each culture in the Weave includes:

- **Culture ID**  
- **Name / Descriptor**  
- **Core Myths**  
- **Norms & Taboos**  
- **Ritual Structures**  
- **Faction Alignments**  
- **Cosmic Interpretations**  
- **Identity & Augmentation Attitudes**  
- **Historical Traumas / Glory Events**  
- **Weave Pattern (threads + tensions)**  

---

## 4. Cultural axes  

Cultures are modeled across **six axes**:

- **Belief Rigidity** — dogmatic ↔ fluid  
- **Cosmic Reverence** — sacred ↔ secular  
- **Augmentation Acceptance** — embraced ↔ feared  
- **Faction Trust** — loyal ↔ cynical  
- **Conflict Normalization** — ritualized ↔ taboo  
- **Memory Stability** — archival ↔ revisionist  

These axes shape how cultures respond to events.

## 6. System behaviors  

### **6.1 Event Interpretation**  
When an event occurs (cosmic, factional, identity, economic, combat), the Weave:

- looks up relevant cultures  
- applies their lenses and threads  
- generates cultural narratives (myths, rumors, doctrines, songs, propaganda)  

### **6.2 Narrative Divergence**  
Different cultures can:

- interpret the same event in conflicting ways  
- generate competing myths and stories  
- create factional tension via narrative framing  

### **6.3 Ritual & Response**  
The Weave defines:

- rituals triggered by cosmic anomalies  
- public responses to Void‑Class events  
- social handling of augmentation crises  
- cultural protocols around conflict and treaties  

### **6.4 Memory & Revision**  
Over time, the Weave:

- archives canonical cultural narratives  
- allows revision, suppression, or mythologizing  
- interacts with the Narrative Continuity Engine to decide what “sticks”

## 7. Cross‑module integration  

- **Cosmology Engine / Cosmic Interaction Model:**  
  - provide raw events; the Weave turns them into meaning.  

- **Narrative Continuity Engine:**  
  - ensures cultural narratives remain coherent with canon.  

- **Story Node Engine & Adventure / Quest System:**  
  - use cultural interpretations to shape quest flavor, stakes, and choices.  

- **Faction Arc Generator:**  
  - faction arcs are reinforced or undermined by cultural narratives.  

- **Sleeve Architecture Spec:**  
  - cultural attitudes toward identity and augmentation affect story pressure.  

- **Combat Physics Model:**  
  - cultural norms define when and how combat is “acceptable” or taboo.

---

## 8. Safety & boundaries  

The Cultural Narrative Weave:

- keeps all cultures fictional and stylized  
- avoids mapping to real‑world cultures or stereotypes  
- respects identity and augmentation safety rails  
- focuses on structural narrative logic, not real‑world sociology
