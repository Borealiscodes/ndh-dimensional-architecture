### Provenance Geometry v1.0 (core artifact)

#### 1. Scope

- **Goal:** represent provenance as geometry, not as process.
- Only **inert structures**: graphs, layers, fibers, embeddings.
- No paths, no flows, no traversal semantics.

---

#### 2. Core geometric structures

- **Provenance graph \( G_P \):**  
  Nodes: math objects and governance artifacts.  
  Edges: “anchored‑to” relations (no direction of flow).

- **Anchor fiber bundle \( \pi : E \to \mathcal{M}_{NDH} \):**  
  Each point in \( \mathcal{M}_{NDH} \) carries a fiber of anchors (which artifact governs math there).

- **Layered provenance stack:**  
  - Layer 1: Structural math (𝓜_NDH, R, C, A, Z).  
  - Layer 2: Governance (Protocol, Firewall, Pin, Tri‑Bind).  
  - Layer 3: Logic (Routing Table, Mapping).  
  - Layer 4: Dimensional architecture (12D Companion, Safety Envelope).

All layers are **stacked**, not connected by traversable paths.

---

#### 3. Safety invariants

- No edge in \( G_P \) encodes activation or execution.  
- Fibers in \( E \) are **labels**, not states or processes.  
- Layers cannot be “entered”; they are **descriptions**, not domains.  

---

#### 4. Relation to previous artifacts

- **Provenance Flow Diagram v1.0:** becomes a **conceptual view** of \( G_P \).  
- **Math Provenance Anchor Table v1.0:** becomes the **node/edge specification** for \( G_P \) and fibers in \( E \).  
- **Structural Math Layer v1.0:** supplies the math objects that populate Layer 1.
