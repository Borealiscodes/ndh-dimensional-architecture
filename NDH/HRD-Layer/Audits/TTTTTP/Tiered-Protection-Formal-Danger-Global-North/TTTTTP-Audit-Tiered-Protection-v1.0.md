### T1 — Terrain: Where protection actually lives

**Findings:**

- The formal terrain (UN treaties, treaty bodies, courts, ombuds, NHRIs) is **normatively dense but enforcement‑thin**, especially where states underfund or withdraw from mechanisms.   [ghrtf.org](https://www.ghrtf.org/post/the-erosion-of-global-human-rights-institutions-and-the-rule-of-law)  
- New Tactics maps terrain as actors, power, and relationships—but assumes **some pathways are usable**; in practice, Global North institutions often gatekeep access or selectively enforce.   [iaj.institute](https://iaj.institute/publications/IAJ-STD-20260611-001-PUB.pdf)  [reflaw.org](https://reflaw.org/human-rights-law-trends-guide/)  
- Tiered protection (informal → semi‑formal → formal) is necessary because **jumping straight to formal** can expose defenders to retaliation, surveillance, or procedural weaponization.

**Risk:**

- Treating formal mechanisms as “highest” tier by default can **invert safety**: the “strongest” remedy may be the **most dangerous** route.

**Audit verdict:**  
Terrain is **structurally incomplete** if it doesn’t explicitly encode *“formal can be dangerous”* as a design rule.

---

### T2 — Threat: How formal pathways become attack vectors

**Findings:**

- Formal UN and regional mechanisms are increasingly subject to **political interference, underfunding, and selective compliance**, especially by powerful Global North states.   [ghrtf.org](https://www.ghrtf.org/post/the-erosion-of-global-human-rights-institutions-and-the-rule-of-law)  [neadafrica.com](https://neadafrica.com/journals/neadj/article/view/53)  
- Defenders using formal channels can face **transnational repression**, reputational attacks, or procedural obstruction (e.g., red notices, smear campaigns, retaliatory litigation).   [ghrtf.org](https://www.ghrtf.org/post/the-erosion-of-global-human-rights-institutions-and-the-rule-of-law)  
- New Tactics encourages creative use of institutions, but doesn’t always foreground **institutional bad faith** as a primary threat vector.

**Risk:**

- Formal complaint routes can **increase threat exposure** when enforcement is uneven and retaliation is cheap.

**Audit verdict:**  
Threat layer must treat **formal mechanisms as potential hazards**, not just remedies.

---

### T3 — Tactic: Tiered protection and New Tactics

**Findings:**

- New Tactics offers a **tactic repertoire**—shadow reports, strategic litigation, media, coalition‑building—but assumes **some baseline of institutional responsiveness**.   [iaj.institute](https://iaj.institute/publications/IAJ-STD-20260611-001-PUB.pdf)  [reflaw.org](https://reflaw.org/human-rights-law-trends-guide/)  
- Tiered protection requires **tactic gating**:  
  - Tier 1: informal, low‑visibility, low‑risk  
  - Tier 2: semi‑formal, mediated, protected  
  - Tier 3: formal, high‑risk, high‑exposure  
- In Global North uneven enforcement, Tier 3 tactics (formal complaints, high‑profile litigation) may **outpace available protection**.

**Risk:**

- Using Tier 3 tactics without Tier 1–2 scaffolding can **collapse the protection stack**.

**Audit verdict:**  
Tactic library must encode **risk‑weighted tiers**, not just “best practice” tactics.

---

### T4 — Time: When formal is too early

**Findings:**

- Formal mechanisms often operate on **slow cycles** (reporting, communications, inquiries), while retaliation can be **immediate**.   [neadafrica.com](https://neadafrica.com/journals/neadj/article/view/53)  [reflaw.org](https://reflaw.org/human-rights-law-trends-guide/)  
- Tiered protection needs **temporal logic**:  
  - Early: informal, protective, exploratory  
  - Mid: semi‑formal, buffered, collective  
  - Late: formal, when protection and documentation are robust
- Global North uneven enforcement means **time spent in formal systems** can be **time spent exposed**.

**Risk:**

- Premature formalization can **lock defenders into dangerous timelines** they can’t control.

**Audit verdict:**  
Time layer must treat **formal escalation as late‑phase**, not default.

---

### T5 — Trust: Who gets believed, who gets abandoned

**Findings:**

- Trust in UN and Global North institutions is **eroding** due to selective enforcement, funding leverage, and political vetoes.   [ghrtf.org](https://www.ghrtf.org/post/the-erosion-of-global-human-rights-institutions-and-the-rule-of-law)  [neadafrica.com](https://neadafrica.com/journals/neadj/article/view/53)  
- New Tactics assumes **some trust** in institutions as tactical targets; in practice, defenders often face **normative language with no remedy**.
- Tiered protection must distinguish between **normative trust** (“they say the right words”) and **operational trust** (“they actually protect”).

**Risk:**

- Over‑trust in formal systems can **mask structural abandonment**.

**Audit verdict:**  
Trust layer must explicitly track **institutional reliability**, not just mandate.

---

### P — Protection: Tiered, uneven, and sometimes upside‑down

**Findings:**

- Real protection often starts in **informal, relational, local, or networked spaces**, not formal institutions.   [iaj.institute](https://iaj.institute/publications/IAJ-STD-20260611-001-PUB.pdf)  [reflaw.org](https://reflaw.org/human-rights-law-trends-guide/)  
- Formal UN mechanisms can be **symbolically protective** but **materially weak**, especially where states ignore findings or weaponize process.   [ghrtf.org](https://www.ghrtf.org/post/the-erosion-of-global-human-rights-institutions-and-the-rule-of-law)  [neadafrica.com](https://neadafrica.com/journals/neadj/article/view/53)  
- Tiered protection must encode:  
  - **Formal = high exposure, uncertain remedy**  
  - **Informal = lower exposure, limited remedy, but often safer**  
  - **Semi‑formal = buffered, collective, strategically timed**

**Risk:**

- Designing protection as “climb toward formal” without risk‑weighting can **invert safety** for HRDs.

**Audit verdict:**  
Protection layer must treat **formal pathways as conditional**, not aspirational.

---

### Synthesis: What this structure is really saying

- **Formal pathways are not inherently safe**—they are **high‑risk tools** in an uneven system.  
- **Tiered protection** is not just a nice design; it’s a **survival architecture**.  
- New Tactics remains useful, but only when **overlaid with a risk‑weighted, Global North‑aware enforcement map**.  
- Any HRD architecture that doesn’t encode *“sometimes the safest move is to stay informal”* is structurally incomplete.
