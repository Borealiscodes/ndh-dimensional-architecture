- **Geometry Evolution**

## Accord Statement  
The Stability Accord is **reciprocal**, **binding**, and **non‑optional**.  
It defines how stability must cooperate with other subsystems — and how those subsystems must cooperate with stability.  
No subsystem may violate or ignore the Accord.

---

# **1. Mutual Obligations**

### **Obligation I — Shared Coherence**  
Stability must maintain coherence, and other subsystems must not introduce incoherence.

### **Obligation II — Shared Predictability**  
Stability must enforce predictability, and other subsystems must not generate unpredictable collapse or traversal.

### **Obligation III — Shared Integrity**  
Stability must preserve structural and semantic integrity, and other subsystems must not distort geometry or meaning.

### **Obligation IV — Shared Safety**  
Stability must ensure safe traversal, and other subsystems must not initiate unsafe transitions.

### **Obligation V — Shared Compatibility**  
Stability must maintain cross‑plane compatibility, and other subsystems must not introduce incompatible states.

# **2. Reciprocal Guarantees**

The Accord guarantees:

- stability will not obstruct lawful subsystem behavior  
- subsystems will not destabilize stability  
- stability will validate subsystem transitions  
- subsystems will provide stability‑relevant metadata  
- stability will record subsystem interactions  
- subsystems will respect stability boundaries  

These guarantees ensure lawful cooperation.

These protections ensure balance.
