# Non-Linear Override Protocol  
### The Constitutional Override Mechanism for Extreme Non-Linear Navigation in the Exploration Quadrant

## 1. Purpose
The Non-Linear Override Protocol defines how explorers may intervene, redirect,
correct, or bypass standard navigation flows inside the Exploration Quadrant
without destabilizing the architecture. It provides a safe override membrane for
extremely non-linear design.

---

## 2. Principle: Overrides Must Be Gentle
All overrides must remain:
- calm
- reversible
- non-destructive
- non-coercive

Gentleness protects the explorer and the system.

---

## 3. Principle: Overrides Must Be Structural
All overrides must respect:
- identity inheritance
- pacing membranes
- boundary membranes
- node alignment
- stillness termination

Structure protects coherence.

---

## 4. Allowed Override Types

### 4.1 Path Override  
Redirect a structural, functional, or narrative path mid-motion.

### 4.2 Node Override  
Insert, skip, or reorder nodes without breaking the chain.

### 4.3 Membrane Override  
Temporarily bypass pacing or boundary membranes.

### 4.4 Diagram Override  
Replace or update visual anchors during navigation.

These are the only sanctioned override types.

---

## 5. Override Requirements

### 5.1 Reversibility  
All overrides must be reversible.  
No override may create irreversible drift.

### 5.2 Stillness Termination  
All overrides must end in Stillness.  
Stillness provides:
- pause  
- breath  
- non-motion  
- gentle coherence  

### 5.3 Non-Destructive Motion  
Overrides may redirect motion but may not collapse layers, break hierarchy, or
invalidate constitutional structures.

### 5.4 Cross-Map Alignment  
Overrides must remain compatible with:
- exploration-crossmap  
- charting-paths-suite  
- exploration-index  
- exploration-core  

Cross-map alignment prevents architectural fragmentation.

---

## 6. Override Flow (Textual)
Trigger Override
↓
Identify Path Type
↓
Apply Node or Membrane Override
↓
Redirect Motion
↓
Terminate in Stillness


This is the safe override loop.

---

## 7. Diagrammatic Requirement
All overrides must be diagrammatically representable.  
Diagrams must:
- follow UMM visual grammar  
- use consistent anchors  
- maintain pacing clarity  
- preserve structural hierarchy  

Diagrammatic clarity prevents drift.

---

## 8. Runtime Considerations
Overrides may be used during:
- exploration  
- navigation  
- diagram updates  
- cross-map traversal  
- non-linear design sessions  

Runtime overrides must remain gentle and reversible.

---

## 9. Summary
The Non-Linear Override Protocol provides a constitutional mechanism for safely
redirecting or correcting navigation inside the Exploration Quadrant. It ensures
that extreme non-linear design remains gentle, reversible, coherent, and
stillness-anchored.

Version: 1.0 (UMM Exploration Protocol)
