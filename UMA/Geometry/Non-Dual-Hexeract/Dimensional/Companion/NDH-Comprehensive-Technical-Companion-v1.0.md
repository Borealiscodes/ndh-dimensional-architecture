## **I. Foundational Ontology: Matter, Anti‑Matter, Universe, Anti‑Universe**

### **1. Matter Ontology**
Matter is defined as a **positive‑existence substrate** with:

- mass‑bearing fields  
- positive‑energy curvature  
- forward‑causal propagation  
- stability‑compatible persistence  

Matter occupies **positive dimensional extension** and supports **Stability**.

### **2. Anti‑Matter Ontology**
Anti‑Matter is a **negative‑existence substrate** with:

- mass‑inverse fields  
- negative‑energy curvature  
- reverse‑causal propagation  
- stadility‑compatible cancellation  

Anti‑Matter occupies **negative dimensional extension** and supports **Stadility**.

### **3. Universe Ontology**
The Universe is a **positive‑existence manifold** with:

- forward‑time continuity  
- positive curvature  
- stability‑aligned field behavior  
- dimensional expansion  

### **4. Anti‑Universe Ontology**
The Anti‑Universe is a **negative‑existence manifold** with:

- reverse‑time continuity  
- negative curvature  
- stadility‑aligned field behavior  
- dimensional contraction

## **II. Stability vs. Stadility: Dual‑Governance Architecture**

### **Stability**
Stability is the **positive‑existence governance layer** ensuring:

- persistence  
- continuity  
- coherence  
- dimensional extension  
- forward‑causal behavior  

### **Stadility**
Stadility is the **negative‑existence governance layer** ensuring:

- cancellation  
- anti‑continuity  
- anti‑coherence  
- dimensional contraction  
- reverse‑causal behavior  

### **Dual‑Governance Interaction**
Stability and Stadility do not interact directly.  
They **anti‑interact**, meaning:

- Stability does not influence Stadility  
- Stadility does not influence Stability  
- Both remain dimensionally isolated  
- Both remain ontologically sealed  

This is governed by the **NDH Anti‑Interaction Principle**.
