## 1. Statement of Principle

**You build the thing.  
You find the ceiling.  
You encode it.**

This principle defines the core method by which UMA‑Universal evolves.  
Every system, structure, or transmission layer is constructed, stress‑tested against its limits, and then extended by encoding those limits directly into the architecture.

---

## 2. Purpose

Ceiling Encoding ensures that:

- no constraint remains invisible  
- no boundary remains implicit  
- no failure mode remains uncharted  
- no traversal path remains unsafe  
- no subsystem operates without declared limits  

Every ceiling becomes part of the map.

---

## 3. Method

### 3.1 Build  
Construct the subsystem, model, or transmission layer with clarity and minimal assumptions.

### 3.2 Find the Ceiling  
Identify the highest constraint the system encounters, including:

- structural limits  
- cognitive limits  
- emotional‑tonal limits  
- chromatic limits  
- rendering limits  
- traversal limits  
- safety limits  

### 3.3 Encode  
Transform the ceiling into architecture by:

- documenting it  
- formalizing it  
- integrating it  
- pinning it  
- exposing it to the Navigation Spine  
- embedding it in protocols and SIDs  

The ceiling becomes a structural feature, not an obstacle.
