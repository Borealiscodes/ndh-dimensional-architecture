## **Core Principles**

- **Principle 1 — Stability First**  
  Governance must protect structural, ethical, and ND‑safe stability before any other concern.

- **Principle 2 — Ethics Over Convenience**  
  No optimization or shortcut may override dignity, justice, or lived experience.

- **Principle 3 — ND‑Safe Pacing**  
  All governance operations must respect humane pacing and avoid overwhelm.

- **Principle 4 — Transparency of Logic**  
  Governance rules must be explicit, inspectable, and non‑mystified.

- **Principle 5 — Self‑Correction**  
  Governance must be able to detect its own drift and restore coherence.

## **Order of Operations**

When any change occurs (layer, workflow, tile, or governance artifact):

1. **Detection**  
   - Unified Auto‑Update Protocol detects change.  

2. **Integrity Check**  
   - BS Kernel evaluates practical, conceptual, emotional, ethical, and structural BS.  

3. **Safety Check**  
   - Safety Interrupt Module evaluates pacing, overwhelm, and ND‑safety.  

4. **Quarantine (if needed)**  
   - Untrusted Archive receives flagged modules.  

5. **Local Auto‑Update**  
   - Layer / Workflow / Tile Map Auto‑Update Protocols update their respective indexes.  

6. **Global Auto‑Update**  
   - Unified Auto‑Update Protocol synchronizes all tiers.  

7. **Stability Evaluation**  
   - Unified Stability Certificate is re‑evaluated.  

8. **Charter Oversight**  
   - Unified Stability Charter ensures all operations respect constitutional limits.

## **Conflict Resolution Rules**

When governance artifacts disagree:

- **Rule 1 — Charter Supremacy**  
  The Unified Stability Charter overrides all other governance artifacts.

- **Rule 2 — Ethics Supremacy**  
  Justice logic overrides convenience, performance, or structural shortcuts.

- **Rule 3 — ND‑Safety Supremacy**  
  Safety Interrupt and ND‑safe pacing override any operation that risks overwhelm.

- **Rule 4 — Integrity Supremacy**  
  BS Kernel flags override attempts to certify or activate unstable modules.

- **Rule 5 — Transparency Supremacy**  
  Hidden or implicit rules are invalid until made explicit in the Codex or Charter.

## **Governance Roles**

- **Charter**  
  - Defines authority, jurisdiction, responsibilities, and limits.  
  - Sets constitutional constraints.

- **Certificate**  
  - Declares current stability status.  
  - Can be active, pending review, or suspended.

- **Protocol**  
  - Executes governance logic (auto‑update, checks, rewrites).  
  - Must obey Charter and Codex.

- **Kernel**  
  - Performs deep integrity checks (BS Kernel).  
  - Has veto power over unstable content.

- **Archive**  
  - Holds quarantined modules.  
  - Prevents contamination of active architecture.

## **Governance Invariants**

These rules must **always** hold:

- **Invariant 1** — No module is active without identity, purpose, activation conditions, relationships, and outputs.  
- **Invariant 2** — No module bypasses BS Kernel checks.  
- **Invariant 3** — No module bypasses ND‑safe pacing and Safety Interrupt.  
- **Invariant 4** — No governance artifact can override ethical protections.  
- **Invariant 5** — Auto‑update protocols must remain active for stability to be valid.  
- **Invariant 6** — All governance logic must be inspectable and documented.

---

## **Drift Handling**

When drift is detected:

1. Mark system status: **“Active — Under Review”**.  
2. Pause non‑essential operations via Safety Interrupt.  
3. Run BS Kernel across affected tiers.  
4. Quarantine unstable modules.  
5. Rebuild relationships and indexes via auto‑update protocols.  
6. Re‑evaluate stability and re‑issue Unified Stability Certificate.

---

## **Extension Rules**

When new governance artifacts are added:

- They must declare:  
  - identity  
  - scope  
  - authority source  
  - limits  
  - relationship to Charter and Codex  

- They must not:  
  - bypass BS Kernel  
  - bypass Safety Interrupt  
  - override Charter clauses  
  - introduce opaque or hidden rules
