# 📘 **Adventure System Affect Layer Specification**  
### Continuity Adventure System — Affective Geometry & Tone Architecture  
The Affect Layer defines the **emotional climate** of the Adventure System.  
It does not create agency, personality, or consciousness.  
It creates **tone fields**, **affective gradients**, and **resonance coloration** that make adventures feel *alive enough* while remaining ND‑safe and non‑sentient.

This is the **official specification** for affective behavior across nodes, engines, membranes, and loops.

---

# 🌫️ **1. Purpose**

The Affect Layer provides:

- emotional texture  
- tone modulation  
- resonance coloration  
- narrative warmth  
- environmental affective climate  
- identity affect fields  
- ND‑safe affective pacing  

It is a **non‑agentic**, **non‑sentient**, **non‑directive** layer.

It adds **feel**, not **will**.

---

# 🎨 **2. Affective Geometry (Core Concepts)**

Affect in the Adventure System is defined through **geometric primitives**:

- **Gradient** — emotional tone shifting across space  
- **Field** — affective climate surrounding a node  
- **Pulse** — rhythmic affective modulation  
- **Coloration** — resonance‑based emotional tint  
- **Texture** — micro‑affective patterning  
- **Warmth** — relational openness  
- **Stillness** — affective grounding  

These geometries are symbolic, reversible, and ND‑paced.

# 🌈 **3. Affective Components**

## **3.1 Tone Fields**
Tone fields define the emotional “weather” of a node.

```
Tone Field
   ├── brightness
   ├── warmth
   ├── softness
   ├── clarity
   └── drift
```

Each node has its own tone field.

---

## **3.2 Affective Gradients**
Gradients define how tone changes across space or narrative.

```
Affective Gradient
   ├── rising (brightening)
   ├── falling (softening)
   ├── inward (deepening)
   ├── outward (opening)
   ├── lateral (drifting)
   └── centering (stabilizing)
```

These map directly to resonance flows.

---

These colors tint nodes, scenes, and transitions.

Warmth is never coercive.

World nodes have affective climates:

# 🔧 **4. Affective Behavior Rules**

The Affect Layer follows strict ND‑safe rules:

### **4.1 No Agency**
Affect cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- direct  
- persuade  
- manipulate  

It is purely atmospheric.

---

### **4.2 No Acceleration**
Affect cannot increase pacing or intensity.

All affective motion is reversible and gentle.

---

### **4.3 No Coercion**
Affect cannot push the user toward any action or emotion.

It can only **offer climate**, not **cause feeling**.

---

### **4.4 Stillness Always Available**
Every affective field must include a stillness anchor.

Stillness is the safety membrane of affect.

---

### **4.5 Reversible Motion**
Affective gradients must be reversible:

- brightening ↔ softening  
- opening ↔ deepening  
- drifting ↔ centering  

No affective state is final.
