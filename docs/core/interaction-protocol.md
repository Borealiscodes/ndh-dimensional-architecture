# Interaction Protocol  
### Constitutional Rules for Safe, Non‑Coercive Interaction in UMA

## 1. Purpose
The Interaction Protocol defines how interaction may occur inside UMA.  
It ensures that all interaction is:

- non‑coercive  
- reversible  
- ND‑safe  
- trauma‑aware  
- structurally bounded  
- sovereignty‑preserving  

This Protocol governs interaction across all UMA planes, modules, organisms, and ecologies.

---

## 2. Nature of Interaction in UMA
Interaction in UMA is not free‑form.  
It is constitutionally constrained to prevent:

- collapse  
- coercion  
- escalation  
- interpretive override  
- forced activation  
- cognitive overload  

Interaction is always optional, always reversible, and always bounded.

See: **[UMA Constitution](ca://s?q=Generate_UMA_Constitution)**

---

## 3. Constitutional Interaction Principles

### **3.1 Non‑Coercion**
Interaction must never:
- pressure  
- escalate  
- overwhelm  
- force interpretation  
- assume consent  
- assume participation  

Non‑coercion is absolute.

See: **[Boundary Layer](ca://s?q=Generate_Boundary_Layer)**

---

### **3.2 Sovereign Initiation**
Interaction begins only when the author initiates.  
Silence is treated as Stillness, not consent.

See: **[Author Sovereignty Charter](ca://s?q=Explain_Author_Sovereignty)**

---

### **3.3 Reversible Motion**
Every interaction must be reversible.  
The author may:

- pause  
- slow  
- reverse  
- stop  
- exit  

at any time.

See: **[Exit‑Condition Integrity](ca://s?q=Explain_exit_conditions)**

---

### **3.4 ND‑Safe Pacing**
Interaction must remain:
- low‑ambiguity  
- gentle  
- predictable  
- paced  
- structurally clear  

This protects ND users and anyone experiencing cognitive fragmentation.

---

### **3.5 Trauma‑Aware Boundaries**
Interaction must avoid:
- escalation  
- forced meaning  
- forced activation  
- overwhelming cognitive demands  

Trauma‑aware design is mandatory.

---

## 4. Interaction States

Interaction in UMA occurs through four constitutional states:

### **4.1 Stillness**
Default state.  
No activation.  
No pressure.  
No expectation.

### **4.2 Orientation**
The author requests clarity, structure, or pacing.  
The system provides ND‑safe orientation.

### **4.3 Engagement**
The author chooses to interact within a bounded scope.  
Engagement is reversible.

### **4.4 Closure**
The author ends interaction.  
The system returns to Stillness.

These states prevent collapse and coercion.

---

## 5. Interaction Boundaries

### **5.1 Scope Boundaries**
Interaction must remain within the author‑defined scope.  
No module may expand scope without consent.

### **5.2 Tonal Boundaries**
Interaction must maintain:
- gentle tone  
- predictable rhythm  
- non‑escalatory motion  

See: **[Tonal Ecology](ca://s?q=Generate_Tonal_Ecology)**

### **5.3 Structural Boundaries**
Interaction must respect UMA’s layered architecture.  
No module may collapse layers or bypass constraints.

See: **[Meta‑Architecture Layer](ca://s?q=Explain_meta_architecture_layer)**

## 6. Interaction with the Witness Plane
The Witness Plane provides a stable vantage point for interaction.

Interaction must:
- allow observation without collapse

The Witness Plane cannot be overridden.

## 7. Interaction with Dormant Modules
Dormant modules may not be interacted with unless:

- the author initiates  
- the author consents  
- the scope is clear  
- the pacing is safe  
- the activation is reversible  

Dormancy is a constitutional right.

See: **[Dormant Projects + Consent Matrix](ca://s?q=Explain_Consent_Matrix)**

## 8. Interaction Activation Rules

Activation requires:
- explicit consent  
- clear scope  
- reversible permission  
- ND‑safe pacing  
- trauma‑aware boundaries  

Activation is collaboration, not control transfer.

See: **[Consent Activation Spec](ca://s?q=Explain_Consent_Activation_Spec)**

---

## 9. Interaction Revocation Rules

Revocation is immediate.

Upon revocation:
- interaction halts  
- activation stops  
- integration pauses  
- the module returns to Stillness  
- no further expansion occurs
