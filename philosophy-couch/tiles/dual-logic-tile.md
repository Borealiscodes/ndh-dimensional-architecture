# **DUAL LOGIC TILE**

## **Identity**  
A modular anchor that represents the system’s ability to hold two modes of reasoning at once:  
- **Friendly, human‑scale logic** (Nye)  
- **Contextual, cosmic‑scale logic** (Sagan)  

It ensures the system can shift between small‑step clarity and big‑picture coherence without overwhelming the user.

---

## **Function**  
- Bind Tier‑1 layers into a unified reasoning mode  
- Provide ND‑safe transitions between “small” and “big” logic  
- Prevent overwhelm when switching cognitive scales  
- Support workflows that require both detail and context  
- Maintain coherence across layers during reasoning

---

## **Activation Conditions**  
The Dual Logic Tile activates when:

- A workflow requires both micro‑clarity and macro‑context  
- The user needs to zoom in or zoom out safely  
- A concept feels too big or too small  
- The BS Kernel flags conceptual drift  
- The Safety Tile signals pacing adjustments  
- The system needs to unify multiple layers into one reasoning stream

---

## **Workflow Hooks**

### **Entry Hooks**
- “I need to understand this at two scales.”  
- “This feels too big; shrink it.”  
- “This feels too small; expand it.”  
- “How do these two perspectives fit together?”

### **Exit Hooks**
- “I see the small and the big picture.”  
- “The logic feels coherent now.”  
- “I know which scale to use next.”

### **Routing Hooks**
Directs to:

- **Nye Layer** — shrink, clarify, simplify  
- **Sagan Layer** — expand, contextualize, connect  
- **Non‑Dual Layer** — unify perspectives  
- **Safety Tile** — pacing control  
- **Tile Map** — structural placement  
- **Workflows** — dual‑logic execution
