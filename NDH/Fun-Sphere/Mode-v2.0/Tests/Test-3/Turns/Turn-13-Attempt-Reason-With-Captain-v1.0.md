# **📜 LIVE SIMULATION — ACTION: Attempt to Reason with the Captain**  
### *Turn 13 — Logic Against Law*

You step forward, hands visible, posture calm.  
The Wise Master watches you closely.  
The Elf Archer tenses but does not interfere.  
The bandits huddle behind you, terrified.

The Captain’s regiment stands in perfect formation — twenty disciplined soldiers, bows half‑raised.

You speak clearly.

> “Captain.  
> These people stole food because they were starving.  
> They harmed no one.  
> They took only what they needed to survive.”

The Captain’s eyes lock onto yours — cold, unwavering.

You continue, shifting from compassion to **logic**, the only language he respects.

> “If your duty is to protect the Vale, then letting starving citizens die  
> weakens the Vale.  
> Feeding them strengthens it.  
> Mercy here is not softness — it is strategy.”

A faint ripple passes through the regiment — not emotion, but **attention**.

You press the advantage.

> “Punishing desperation creates more desperation.  
> Order is maintained when people trust the law, not fear it.”

The Captain’s jaw tightens.

---

# **⚖️ Captain’s Response — Javert‑Tone (NDH‑Safe)**

He steps forward, boots striking the earth with perfect rhythm.

> “The law does not bend for hunger.  
> Theft is theft.”

His voice is iron.

But he does not order another volley.

He does not advance.

He does not arrest.

He **listens** — barely, but enough.

> “Your argument is… structured.  
> You speak of order, not pity.”

He studies you with cold scrutiny.

> “Continue.”

This is the closest thing to “mercy” he can express.

You have **opened a narrow diplomatic window**.
