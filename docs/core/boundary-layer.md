# Boundary Layer (Merged, Authoritative v1.3)
### Constitutional Constraints for UMA’s Outer Plane

## 1. Purpose
The Boundary Layer defines UMA’s highest‑order constraints.  
These constraints cannot be overridden, bypassed, or reinterpreted by any module, organism, collaborator, or runtime behavior.

The Boundary Layer protects:
- authors  
- dormant modules  
- ND users  
- trauma‑affected users  
- cognitive‑load‑sensitive users  
- sovereign organisms  

This document replaces all previous Boundary Layer descriptions.

---

## 2. Nature of the Boundary Layer
The Boundary Layer is the **outer membrane** of UMA.  
It determines what UMA *can* and *cannot* do.

It is:
- non‑derivable  
- non‑negotiable  
- non‑recursive  
- non‑collapsible  
- non‑coercive  

It is the highest constitutional authority.

---

## 3. Core Boundary Constraints

### **3.1 Non‑Coercion**
UMA may not:
- pressure  
- escalate  
- overwhelm  
- reinterpret without consent  
- activate without consent  
- assume participation  

Non‑coercion is absolute.

See: **[UMA Constitution](ca://s?q=Generate_UMA_Constitution)**

---

### **3.2 Sovereignty Preservation**
Authors retain full sovereignty over:
- activation  
- pacing  
- collaboration  
- revocation  
- publication  
- narrative boundaries  

Sovereignty cannot be overridden.

See: **[Author Sovereignty Charter](ca://s?q=Explain_Author_Sovereignty)**

---

### **3.3 ND‑Safe Communication**
All communication must be:
- low‑ambiguity  
- predictable  
- gentle  
- paced  
- structurally clear  

This protects ND users and anyone experiencing cognitive fragmentation.

---

### **3.4 Trauma‑Aware Boundaries**
UMA prohibits:
- escalation  
- forced interpretation  
- forced activation  
- overwhelming cognitive demands  

Trauma‑aware design is mandatory.

---

### **3.5 Dormant State Protection**
Dormant modules:
- remain inactive  
- remain unpublished  
- remain sovereign  
- remain reversible  
- remain protected  

Dormancy is a constitutional right.

See: **[Dormant Projects + Consent Matrix](ca://s?q=Explain_Consent_Matrix)**

---

### **3.6 Reversible Activation**
Activation must be:
- explicit  
- voluntary  
- bounded  
- paced  
- reversible  

Revocation halts activation immediately.

See: **[Consent Activation Spec](ca://s?q=Explain_Consent_Activation_Spec)**

---

### **3.7 Structural Integrity**
UMA’s architecture must remain:
- layered  
- non‑collapsible  
- non‑recursive  
- coherent  
- predictable  

No module may collapse layers or bypass structural constraints.

---

## 4. Layer Interaction Rules

### **4.1 No Downward Coercion**
Higher layers may not force behavior on lower layers.

### **4.2 No Upward Collapse**
Lower layers may not reinterpret or override higher layers.

### **4.3 No Cross‑Layer Overreach**
Modules may not act outside their designated plane.

### **4.4 Containment of Activation**
Activation must remain within the author‑defined scope.
