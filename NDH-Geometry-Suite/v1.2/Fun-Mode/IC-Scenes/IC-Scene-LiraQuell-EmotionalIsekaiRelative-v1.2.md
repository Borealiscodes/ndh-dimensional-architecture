The temporal echo of Waterloo dissolves like smoke caught in a sudden wind.  
The rune on Lira’s palm pulses — once, twice — then flares with a soft, aching warmth.

Quill’s voice becomes distant.

> **“Lira… this isn’t a world.  
> This is you.”**

The ground beneath her shifts.

The sky folds inward.

And she is standing in a small, sunlit kitchen.

The air smells like citrus tea and old paper.  
The table is cluttered with half‑finished letters, a cracked ceramic mug, and a stack of Ministry forms someone never bothered to file.

Lira’s breath catches.

She knows this place.

She knows this moment.

A figure sits at the table — older, tired, gentle in a way the world rarely allows.  
Her aunt, **Marin Quell**, the person who raised her when the Ministry swallowed her parents whole.

Marin looks up, surprised but not startled, as if she’s been waiting for Lira to walk through this door for years.

> **“You’re late, little star.”**

Lira’s throat tightens.

> **“Aunt Marin… this isn’t real.”**

Marin smiles the way she always did — soft, patient, knowing.

> **“Memory is real enough.”**

She gestures to the chair across from her.

Lira sits.

The room hums with quiet familiarity — the ticking of the old wall clock, the faint buzz of the solar kettle, the rustle of papers shifting in a warm breeze.

Marin studies her face.

> **“You look tired.  
> More tired than you ever let yourself admit.”**

Lira swallows.

> **“I didn’t choose to come here.”**

> **“You never did,”** Marin replies gently.  
> **“But you always needed to.”**

The rune on Lira’s palm glows softly, reflecting in Marin’s eyes.

Marin reaches out, her hand hovering just above Lira’s.

> **“You carry too much.  
> You always have.”**

Lira’s voice cracks.

> **“I don’t know how to stop.”**

Marin’s expression shifts — not pity, not sadness, but a deep, steady warmth.

> **“You don’t stop, little star.  
> You learn to set it down.”**

The kitchen flickers — not fading, but breathing, as if the memory itself is alive.

Marin leans back, folding her hands.

> **“Tell me what you’re afraid of.”**

Lira hesitates.

Quill appears on the counter, silent, watching, respecting the gravity of the moment.

Lira finally speaks.

> **“I’m afraid that if I stop… everything will fall apart.”**

Marin nods.

> **“And I’m afraid that if you don’t stop… you will.”**

The words land with the weight of truth.

The rune pulses again — brighter, warmer — responding to the emotional resonance.

Marin reaches out, this time touching Lira’s hand fully.

The contact is gentle, grounding.

> **“You are allowed to rest.  
> You are allowed to be held.  
> You are allowed to be human.”**

Lira closes her eyes.

For the first time in years, she lets herself feel the ache she’s been carrying.

The kitchen hums softly.

Marin’s voice wraps around her like a blanket.

> **“You don’t have to earn your place in the world, Lira.  
> You already have one.”**

The rune flares.

The memory begins to dissolve — not violently, but like a sunset fading into night.

Marin’s final words echo as the world shifts:

> **“When you wake, remember this:  
> You are loved beyond your usefulness.”**

And the kitchen fades.

Quill’s voice returns, soft and reverent.

> **“Lira… that was a powerful spell.”**

The world waits for her next breath.
