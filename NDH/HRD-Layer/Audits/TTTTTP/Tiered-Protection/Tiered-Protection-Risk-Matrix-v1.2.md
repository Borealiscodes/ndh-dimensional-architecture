# **📘 Matrix Interpretation (Governed NDH‑Safe)**

## **Tier 1 — Informal Safety**
- **Exposure:** minimal  
- **Retaliation:** minimal  
- **Enforcement:** not required  
- **Institutional behavior:** relational, trust‑based  
- **Risk:** lowest  
- **Use:** early detection, quiet documentation, Ombuds‑style neutrality  

**Runtime Lattice:** monitors for escalation triggers.

---

## **Tier 2 — Semi‑Formal Buffer**
- **Exposure:** moderate  
- **Retaliation:** diffused  
- **Enforcement:** uneven (regional soft mechanisms)  
- **Institutional behavior:** buffered, mediated  
- **Risk:** manageable  
- **Use:** coalition reporting, protected intermediaries  

**Runtime Lattice:** checks for safe transition to Tier 3.

---

## **Tier 3 — Formal Mechanisms**
- **Exposure:** high  
- **Retaliation:** direct and severe  
- **Enforcement:** uneven–weak (UN systems)  
- **Institutional behavior:** procedural gatekeeping  
- **Risk:** high  
- **Use:** UN communications, litigation, national complaints  

**UC:** prevents premature escalation.  
**Runtime Lattice:** blocks unsafe transitions.

---

## **Tier 4 — Global Formal Escalation**
- **Exposure:** extreme  
- **Retaliation:** systemic  
- **Enforcement:** highly uneven (Global North asymmetry)  
- **Institutional behavior:** political, strategic  
- **Risk:** existential  
- **Use:** global advocacy, media amplification, Security Council attention  

**UC + Runtime Lattice:** enforce strict gating.

---

# **📘 Cross‑Tier Risk Dynamics (v1.2 Enhancements)**

### **1. Exposure Risk**
- Increases exponentially from Tier 1 → Tier 4  
- Formal pathways amplify visibility faster than protection can scale

### **2. Retaliation Risk**
- Moves from interpersonal → institutional → systemic  
- Global North uneven enforcement increases Tier 3–4 retaliation risk

### **3. Enforcement Reliability**
- Tier 1–2: local/regional, moderately reliable  
- Tier 3–4: UN systems, highly uneven, politically influenced

### **4. Institutional Behavior**
- Tier 1: relational  
- Tier 2: buffered  
- Tier 3: procedural  
- Tier 4: political  

This is the **core danger**:  
Formal mechanisms behave politically, not neutrally.

- Runtime Lattice + UC enforce **safe tier transitions**
