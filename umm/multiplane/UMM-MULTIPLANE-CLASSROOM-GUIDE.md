# 🌱 **1. Multiplane Teaching Principles**
Multiplane teaching follows five constitutional principles:

- **Reversible** — every multiplane action can be undone  
- **Symbolic** — nothing is literal or emotional  
- **ND‑paced** — slow, predictable, gentle  
- **Non‑coercive** — participation optional  
- **Non‑interpretive** — no meaning assigned to actions  

These principles protect learners during multiplane work.

---

# 🧭 **2. Instructor Multiplane Compass**
Before teaching multiplane interaction, perform a Compass check.

### **North — Structure**  
Name the two planes you will pair.

### **South — Safety**  
Ensure the environment is calm and predictable.

### **East — Tone**  
Choose a gentle tone for both planes.

### **West — Cosmology**  
Keep cosmology outside multiplane work.

### **Center — Pacing**  
Check the group’s pacing.

Use:  
- **Compass North**  
- **Compass South**  
- **Compass East**  
- **Compass West**  
- **Compass Center**  

---

# 🗺️ **3. Choosing Safe Plane Pairs**
Beginners should only pair **two planes at a time**.

### **Recommended Classroom Pairs**
- **Map ↔ World**  
- **World ↔ Character**  
- **Map ↔ Play Tree**  
- **Character ↔ Play Tree**

### **Avoid in Classroom**
- three‑plane pairs  
- cosmology pairs  
- rapid switching  
- emotional interpretation  

Plane pairing keeps the classroom stable.

# 🌈 **8. Multiplane Drift Prevention**
To prevent drift during multiplane teaching:

### **Instructor Actions**
- reduce the number of planes  
- shrink the field  
- return to one symbol  
- pause between planes  
- check Center often  

Use: **Compass Pacing Regulator**

# ⭐ **9. Multiplane Zen Reset**
If the classroom becomes overwhelmed:

### **Reset Flow**
- one breath  
- one anchor dot  
- one shape  
- one tone  

Use: **Compass Zen Reset**

This resets all planes gently.

# 🌟 **10. Full Multiplane Classroom Session**
A complete gentle multiplane session:

1. **Stillness**  
2. Compass check  
3. Choose two planes  
4. Draw a soft map  
5. Enter one world  
6. Meet one character  
7. Walk one reversible branch  
8. Add one soft detail

11. Close the field softly  

This is the safest multiplane teaching loop.

# 📘 **11. Instructor Troubleshooting**
### **If the group feels overwhelmed:**  
- shrink the field  
- reduce to one symbol  
- pause  
- reset  

### **If multiplane drift occurs:**  
- return to Compass  
- simplify the map  
- remove world details  

### **If pacing collapses:**  
- Zen Reset  
- remove symbols  
- slow transitions  

### **If someone needs space:**  
- allow quiet participation  
- avoid spotlighting  
- offer reversible actions
