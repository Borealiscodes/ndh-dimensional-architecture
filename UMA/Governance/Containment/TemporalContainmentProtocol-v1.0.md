This protocol governs **safe temporal traversal** across lifecycle phases, adjacency edges, and containment gates.  
It ensures that subsystem state transitions remain stable, ethical, lineage‑consistent, and bleed‑controlled.

---

## **1. Purpose**

Temporal containment prevents:

- phase‑bleed  
- IC/OOC drift  
- traversal contamination  
- unstable rollback  
- cross‑subsystem temporal leakage  
- identity fragmentation  
- lineage discontinuity  

It is the **temporal safety layer** of UMA’s spine.

---

## **2. Containment Preconditions**

Traversal may begin only if:

- **SIAP routing** is stable  
- **CHS‑OL** is active  
- **Play Engine containment** is verified  
- **HRD constraints** are satisfied  
- **HBR stability** is confirmed  
- **Roots lineage** supports the transition  

If any condition fails → **temporal traversal is blocked**.

---

## **3. Temporal Containment Gates**

Temporal containment uses three gate types:

### **3.1 Structural Gate**  
Ensures traversal respects:

- subsystem boundaries  
- adjacency edges  
- SIAP routing rules  

**Anchoring Pins:**  
- **PIN‑002** (SIAP)  
- **PIN‑070/071** (Adjacency Matrix)

---

### **3.2 Ethical Gate**  
Ensures traversal does not violate HRD constraints.

HRD overrides SIAP.

**Anchoring Pin:**  
- **PIN‑021** (HRD)

---

### **3.3 Bleed‑Control Gate**  
Ensures IC/OOC transitions remain stable.

**Anchoring Pins:**  
- **PIN‑050–052** (Play Engine)  
- **PIN‑024** (Bleed‑Control Verification)

---

## **4. Temporal Containment Sequence**

```
Precondition Check
   ↓
Structural Gate (SIAP + Adjacency)
   ↓
Ethical Gate (HRD)
   ↓
Bleed-Control Gate (Play Engine)
   ↓
Temporal Traversal (Forward / Lateral / Reverse)
   ↓
Containment Revalidation
   ↓
Roots Lineage Update
   ↓
Lifecycle Phase Update
```

This ensures traversal is safe, stable, and lineage‑consistent.

---

## **5. Containment Modes**

### **5.1 Forward Containment (Allowed)**  
Used during normal lifecycle progression.

- Genesis → Initialization  
- Activation → Operation  
- Operation → Extension  

Forward traversal is the safest mode.

---

### **5.2 Lateral Containment (Conditional)**  
Used for traversal between subsystems at the same temporal phase.

Allowed only if:

- adjacency edges are valid  
- containment gates are stable  
- traversal plane is active  

Example:  
CHS‑OL ↔ Play Engine during Operation.

---

### **5.3 Reverse Containment (Restricted)**  
Used for rollback or deactivation.

Allowed only when:

- subsystem instability detected  
- containment gates locked  
- lineage continuity preserved  

Reverse traversal is tightly controlled.

---

## **6. Temporal Drift Prevention**

Temporal drift is prevented by:

- **Roots lineage anchoring**  
- **continuity enforcement**  
- **identity stabilization**  
- **adjacency constraints**  
- **containment gates**  
- **HRD overrides**  

If drift is detected → traversal halts and subsystem enters rollback mode.

## **7. Subsystem‑Specific Containment Rules**

### **7.1 UMA Core**  
Always stable.  
Never subject to containment.

### **7.2 SIAP Spine**  
Must remain temporally continuous.  
Routing locks during containment.

### **7.3 CHS / CHS‑OL**  
CHS‑OL handles temporal traversal.  
CHS geometry not required for containment.

### **7.4 Play Engine**  
Primary containment subsystem.  
All IC/OOC transitions pass through it.

### **7.5 UMM / HRD**  
HRD enforces ethical containment.  
UMM provides structural envelope.

### **7.6 Roots**  
All temporal transitions update lineage.  
Roots prevents identity drift.

### **7.7 Lifecycle Chain**  
Controls temporal sequencing.  
Rollback rules enforced here.

- adjacency constraints

- stability audits  
- ethical overrides  
- lineage anchoring  

Full hypercube geometry is **not required**.

The spine works.
