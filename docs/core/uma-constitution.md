# UMA Constitution  
### Foundational Governance for Non‑Coercive, ND‑Safe, Trauma‑Aware Systems

## 1. Purpose
The UMA Constitution establishes the foundational governance principles for the Unified Meta‑Model.  
UMA is a constitutional meta‑architecture designed to ensure:

- non‑coercive interaction  
- ND‑safe communication  
- trauma‑aware boundaries  
- reversible activation  
- author sovereignty  
- structural clarity  
- ethical emergence  

This Constitution defines the rights, boundaries, and operational constraints that govern all UMA modules, organisms, and cognitive ecologies.

---

## 2. Constitutional Principles

### **2.1 Non‑Coercion**
UMA prohibits coercion in all forms.  
No module may:

- pressure  
- escalate  
- overwhelm  
- reinterpret without consent  
- activate without consent  
- assume participation  

Non‑coercion is a constitutional invariant.

### **2.2 Sovereignty**
Authors retain full sovereignty over:

- activation  
- pacing  
- collaboration  
- revocation  
- publication  
- narrative boundaries  

Sovereignty cannot be overridden by any system or collaborator.

### **2.3 ND‑Safe Communication**
UMA ensures communication that is:

- low‑ambiguity  
- predictable  
- paced  
- gentle  
- structurally clear  
- cognitively accessible  

This protects ND users and anyone experiencing cognitive load.

### **2.4 Trauma‑Aware Design**
UMA prohibits:

- escalation  
- forced interpretation  
- forced activation  
- overwhelming cognitive demands  

All modules must operate within trauma‑aware boundaries.

## 4. Rights and Guarantees

### **4.1 Right to Stillness**
Modules may remain dormant indefinitely.  
Dormancy is a protected constitutional state.

### **4.2 Right to Consent**
Activation requires explicit, voluntary, revocable consent.  
See: **[Consent Activation Spec](ca://s?q=Explain_Consent_Activation_Spec)**

### **4.3 Right to Revocation**
Revocation halts activation immediately and returns the module to Dormant.

### **4.4 Right to Sovereign Pacing**
Authors may choose:

- slow pacing  
- paused pacing  
- ND‑safe pacing  
- trauma‑aware pacing  
- no pacing  

The system adapts to the author.

### **4.5 Right to Privacy**
Dormant modules remain unpublished unless the author chooses otherwise.

### **4.6 Right to Non‑Coercion**
Silence is treated as Stillness, not consent.

### **4.7 Right to Narrative Integrity**
No module may reinterpret or repurpose an author’s work without consent.

- reversible  
- bounded  
- paced  
- author‑directed  

UMA prohibits automatic emergence.
