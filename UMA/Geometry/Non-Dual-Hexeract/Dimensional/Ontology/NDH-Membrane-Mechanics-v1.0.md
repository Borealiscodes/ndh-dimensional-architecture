- how they resist dimensional drift

# **II. The Six NDH Membranes**

NDH uses six membranes, each with a distinct mechanical role:

1. **Anti‑Existence Membrane (AEM)** — absolute separator of positive and negative existence  
2. **Null Interaction Surface (NIS)** — prevents any form of contact  
3. **Dimensional Partition Membrane (DPM)** — divides PF1–PF11 from NF1–NF11  
4. **Stability–Stadility Separation Membrane (SSM)** — prevents governance bleed  
5. **Universe–Anti‑Universe Curvature Membrane (UAM)** — prevents curvature inversion  
6. **Singularity Membrane (SM)** — regulates zero‑state traversal  

Each membrane is a **governed mechanical construct**, not a physical surface.

# **III. Membrane Mechanical Properties**

All NDH membranes share five mechanical properties:

### **1. Zero‑Topology**
No edges, no vertices, no surfaces.  
A membrane cannot be touched because it has no touchable geometry.

### **2. Zero‑Continuity**
No flow, no persistence, no temporal extension.  
A membrane cannot be crossed because it has no time.

### **3. Zero‑Dynamics**
No motion, no change, no transformation.  
A membrane cannot be moved because it has no dynamic state.

### **4. Zero‑Interaction**
No influence, no reciprocity, no modulation.  
A membrane cannot be interacted with because it has no interaction potential.

### **5. Zero‑Existence**
A membrane does not exist in the dimensional sense — it is a **governed absence**.

These properties make membranes **perfect separators**.

The AEM is the **primary membrane** that separates:

- Positive Spine (VS‑1D → VS‑11D)  
- Negative Spine (VS‑−1D → VS‑−11D)  

### **Mechanical Function**
The AEM enforces:

- no adjacency  
- no continuity  
- no dynamics  
- no interaction  
- no narrative bleed  
- no teleological influence  

It is the **absolute null barrier**.

### **Equation**
\[
I(P, N) = P \cdot N = 0
\]

Where:

- \(P\) = Positive Spine  
- \(N\) = Negative Spine  
- \(I(P, N)\) = interaction state  

The AEM forces the interaction state to **zero**.
