# **🟧 IC Scene — Lira Quell Isekai Transition: Arrival in Aetherion**

Sector 7‑L dissolves.

Not with heat.  
Not with light.  
But with **paperwork**.

The Emberling’s meltdown reaches its symbolic peak, and the Ministry’s atrium folds inward like a collapsing origami crane. Lira feels the floor vanish beneath her boots, replaced by a sensation like falling through a stack of unfinished forms.

A single line of text flashes across her vision:

> **“Dimensional Routing: Approved.”**

Then—

A rush of wind.  
A burst of color.  
A sound like a choir made of bells and thunder.

And Lira lands—  
not on metal, not on coral‑grown flooring,  
but on **grass**.

Grass that glows faintly blue.

Grass that hums.

Grass that rearranges itself politely to cushion her fall.

She sits up, blinking.

The sky above her is a swirling tapestry of floating islands, drifting like slow, thoughtful whales. Rivers of light flow between them, forming luminous bridges that pulse with arcane rhythm.

A towering crystalline tree rises nearby, its branches fractal and infinite, each leaf inscribed with shifting runes.

The air tastes like sunlight and ink.

Lira whispers:

> **“This is… not Sector 7‑L.”**

The world answers.

---

## **🟧 NPC 1 — The Aetherion Gate Warden**

A figure steps from behind the crystalline tree, wearing robes woven from starlight and parchment. Their eyes glow with the same runes that shimmer across the leaves.

They speak with a voice like a librarian who moonlights as a thunderstorm:

> **“Traveler of the Bureau of Probable Tomorrows… welcome to Aetherion.”**

They bow.

> **“I am the Gate Warden.  
> You have arrived through a sanctioned narrative ignition.  
> Your presence is… anomalous, but permitted.”**

The Warden gestures, and the runes on the tree rearrange themselves into a sigil that looks suspiciously like a Ministry stamp.

---

## **🟧 NPC 2 — The Familiar Who Chose Her**

A small creature emerges from the grass—  
a fox‑like being made of swirling ink and light, its tail splitting into three elegant brushstrokes.

It circles Lira, sniffing her coat pockets.

Her sun‑seed pulses in response.

The creature sits, tail curling neatly.

> **“I choose this one.”**

The Warden raises an eyebrow.

> **“Already? You usually take weeks.”**

The creature shrugs.

> **“She smells like unfinished paperwork.  
> That means destiny.”**

Lira stares.

> **“I… what?”**

The creature hops onto her shoulder.

> **“I am Quill. Your familiar.  
> You will need me.”**

---

## **🟧 NPC 3 — The Apprentice Mage Who Saw the Sky Tear**

A young mage sprints toward them, robes flapping wildly, clutching a staff that appears to be half‑grown, half‑forged.

They skid to a stop.

> **“Gate Warden! The sky just opened and dropped a bureaucrat!”**

Lira raises a hand.

> **“I’m not— well, technically— but also—”**

The mage leans in, eyes sparkling.

> **“Do you know magic?”**

Lira opens her mouth to say no.

The crystalline tree answers for her.

A single rune detaches from a leaf, floats downward, and lands gently on her palm.

It glows.

It hums.

It sinks into her skin like warm ink.

The mage gasps.

> **“She’s been marked by the Aether Script!”**

The Warden nods slowly.

> **“It seems the world has chosen her.”**

Quill flicks its tail proudly.

> **“Told you.”**

---

## **🟧 Magic System Reveal — The Aether Script**

The rune inside Lira’s palm unfurls into a pattern of glowing lines that wrap around her wrist like a living bracelet.

The Warden explains:

> **“Magic in Aetherion is written, not cast.  
> Every spell is a sentence.  
> Every sentence is a negotiation with reality.”**

The mage adds:

> **“And the Script only marks those who can rewrite probability.”**

Lira blinks.

Her sun‑seed pulses.

Her coat pockets rustle.

She mutters:

> **“Right.  
> New world.  
> New rules.  
> And apparently… new paperwork.”**

Quill nods.

> **“Welcome to Aetherion.”**

The sky shifts overhead, islands drifting into new constellations.

The world begins building itself around her.
