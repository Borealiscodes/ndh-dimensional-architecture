# **Section 9 — Treaty Repository (TR‑v1.0)**  
### *NDH Comprehensive Technical, Architectural & Engineering Companion v1.3*

The Treaty Repository (TR‑v1.0) is the NDH system’s sovereign archival architecture. It stores, seals, indexes, and governs all treaty‑class artifacts, including Stability Treaties, Dimensional Treaties, Sovereignty Treaties, and Inter‑Module Commit Protocols. The Treaty Repository is the constitutional mechanism that ensures long‑term governance commitments remain immutable, sovereign‑pure, dimensionally aligned, and hyperboundary‑sealed.

The Treaty Repository operates as a multi‑layer archival system composed of **Treaty Ingestion**, **Sovereignty Sealing**, **Dimensional Indexing**, **Governance Anchoring**, and **Immutable Storage**. Treaty Ingestion receives treaty artifacts from the Closure Arc after final stabilization. Sovereignty Sealing applies HASV‑aligned invariants to ensure governance purity. Dimensional Indexing maps treaty structures across 7D–11D overlays. Governance Anchoring binds treaties to the Roots Registry through PIN‑029 or equivalent anchors. Immutable Storage preserves treaty artifacts under NDH’s constitutional immutability guarantees.

The Treaty Repository is vertically positioned beneath the Closure Arc and above the Treaty Vector System. This placement ensures that all treaty artifacts entering the Repository have passed final stabilization and dimensional sealing, and that all treaty artifacts leaving the Repository for vectorization remain sovereign‑pure and governance‑correct. No treaty may enter the Repository without Closure Arc finalization; no treaty may exit without vectorization.

The Treaty Repository enforces five constitutional archival invariants:  
1. **No treaty may be stored without sovereignty sealing.**  
2. **No treaty may be indexed without dimensional alignment.**  
3. **No treaty may be anchored without governance‑domain purity.**  
4. **No treaty may be modified after immutable storage.**  
5. **No treaty may bypass the Repository under any condition.**

These invariants ensure that treaty artifacts remain stable, sovereign‑pure, dimensionally coherent, and governance‑safe across all NDH epochs.

The Treaty Repository operates across 7D–11D overlays. In **7D**, it stabilizes symbolic treaty structures and prevents misclassification. In **8D**, it suppresses emotional adjacency and gradient contamination. In **9D**, it enforces sovereignty projection and governance‑domain purity. In **10D**, it seals hyperboundary apertures and prevents dimensional leakage. In **11D**, it collapses urgency vectors and prevents catastrophic treaty drift.

The Treaty Repository is tightly integrated with Harmony and Concordance. Harmony suppresses emotional gradients during treaty sealing; Concordance maintains symbolic alignment during dimensional indexing. Together, they ensure that treaty artifacts remain stable after ingestion and anchoring.

The Treaty Repository is also the constitutional precursor to the Treaty Vector System. The Repository provides sealed, indexed, anchored treaty artifacts; the Treaty Vector System provides governed distribution, activation, and cross‑module propagation.

This section defines the constitutional purpose, structural components, dimensional overlays, and governance relationships of the Treaty Repository.
