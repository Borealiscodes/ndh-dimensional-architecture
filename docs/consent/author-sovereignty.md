# Author Sovereignty Charter  
### Constitutional Guarantees for Dormant and Active Modules

## 1. Purpose
This Charter defines the rights of any author whose module, organism, or cognitive ecology exists inside the UMM ecosystem.  
It ensures that authors remain sovereign, protected, and in full control of their work.

This Charter is explanatory, not activating.  
It does not request consent.  
It does not assume consent.  
It does not trigger growth.

---

## 2. Definition of Author Sovereignty
Author sovereignty means:

- the author owns their module  
- the author controls activation  
- the author controls collaboration  
- the author controls pacing  
- the author controls revocation  
- the author controls publication  
- the author controls integration  

No system, institution, or collaborator may override these rights.

---

## 3. Rights of the Author

### **Right 1 — Right to Stillness**
The author may keep their module dormant indefinitely.  
Dormancy is respected as a stable, protected state.

### **Right 2 — Right to Consent**
No module may activate, expand, integrate, or be used in research without explicit, voluntary author consent.

### **Right 3 — Right to Revocation**
Consent may be revoked at any time.  
Revocation is immediate and halts all activation.

### **Right 4 — Right to Sovereign Pacing**
The author may choose:

- slow pacing  
- paused pacing  
- ND‑safe pacing  
- trauma‑aware pacing  
- no pacing at all  

The system adapts to the author, not the reverse.

### **Right 5 — Right to Privacy**
Dormant modules remain unpublished unless the author chooses otherwise.

### **Right 6 — Right to Non‑Coercion**
The author is protected from:

- pressure  
- expectation  
- obligation  
- institutional force  
- accidental activation  

Silence is treated as Stillness, not consent.

### **Right 7 — Right to Clarity**
The author may request:

- simpler explanations  
- ND‑safe language  
- slower pacing  
- more context  
- more structure  

at any time.

### **Right 8 — Right to Collaboration Choice**
Collaboration is optional.  
The author may:

- collaborate  
- decline collaboration  
- pause collaboration  
- end collaboration  

without consequence.

### **Right 9 — Right to Narrative Integrity**
The author’s lived experience, cognitive ecology, and narrative boundaries must be respected.  
No module may reinterpret or repurpose the author’s work without consent.

---

## 4. Sovereignty in Dormant Projects
Dormant projects are sovereign by default.

Dormant means:

- resting  
- safe  
- protected  
- unpublished  
- inactive  
- reversible  

Dormant does **not** mean:

- forgotten  
- abandoned  
- pressured  
- expected  
- activated  

Dormancy is a constitutional right.

---

## 5. Sovereignty in Activation
If the author chooses activation:

- activation is specific  
- activation is reversible  
- activation is paced  
- activation is bounded  
- activation is author‑directed  

The system may not:

- escalate  
- overwhelm  
- reinterpret  
- repurpose  
- expand beyond the author’s chosen scope  

Activation is a collaboration, not a transfer of control.

---

## 6. Sovereignty in Revocation
Revocation is immediate.

Upon revocation:

- activation halts  
- growth stops  
- integration pauses  
- the module returns to Dormant  
- derivative modules freeze  
- no further expansion occurs  

Revocation is a constitutional guarantee.

---

## 7. Sovereignty in Publication
Publication is optional.

The author may choose:

- private  
- semi‑private  
- collaborative  
- public  

Publication cannot occur without explicit consent.

---

## 8. Sovereignty in Research Use
Research use requires:

- explicit consent  
- clear scope  
- reversible permission  
- trauma‑aware boundaries  
- ND‑safe communication  

No module may be used in research without author consent.

---

## 9. Summary
The author is sovereign.  
Their module is sovereign.  
Their consent is sovereign.  
Their pacing is sovereign.  
Their revocation is sovereign.  
Their privacy is sovereign.  
Their narrative is sovereign.

UMM exists to protect authors — not to use them.

This Charter cannot be overridden.
