# **TPS Glossary (v1.0)**  
### *Definitions for the Two‑Pin System and SIAP Governance*

**System:** UMA Universal  
**Subsystem:** SIAP — System Integrity Alignment Protocol  
**Document Type:** Glossary / Semantic Layer  
**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** 03 July 2026

---

## **A — Anchoring & Architecture**

### **Anchor**  
A formal stabilization point inside UMA’s conceptual architecture. Anchors prevent drift, collapse, or misalignment across layers.

### **Astrolabe**  
The inner navigational instrument of UMA. The first module to be pinned (AP‑01).

### **Architecture Ring**  
The layer of UMA concerned with structural components, modules, and their relationships.

---

## **C — Cosmic & Contextual Terms**

### **Cosmic Architecture**  
The full multi‑layer UMA cosmos (Heliosphere, Dyson Sphere, Luna Base, Cosmic Forest). Not yet eligible for anchoring.

### **Context Ring**  
The outermost interpretive layer: world, device, user, environment.

### **Cognitive Load**  
The mental effort required to interpret a module. Must remain safe for anchoring.

---

## **D — Dual‑Pin System**

### **Dual‑Anchor Model**  
The structural principle behind TPS: every module requires both a Map Pin and a SIAP Pin.

---

## **E — Evaluation & Ethics**

### **Ethics Core**  
The center of UMA’s conceptual engine. All modules must align with it.

### **Evaluation (SIAP)**  
The process by which SIAP determines whether a module is eligible for anchoring.

---

## **F — Failures & Flow**

### **Failure Mode**  
Any condition that causes a module to collapse, bunch, drift, or overload. Failure modes trigger SIAP vetoes.

### **Flow Integrity**  
The ability of a module to maintain stable relationships across layers.

---

## **G — Governance**

### **Governance Layer**  
The SIAP subsystem responsible for integrity, pacing, safety, and anchoring decisions.

### **Governance Drift**  
When rules or decisions become inconsistent across modules. TPS prevents this.

---

## **H — Heliosphere**

### **Heliosphere**  
The containment layer surrounding the Astrolabe. Next module eligible for future anchoring (HS‑01).

---

## **I — Integrity & Indexing**

### **Integrity Anchor**  
The SIAP Pin that records approval, rationale, pacing, and safety.

### **Index (TPS)**  
The directory map for the TPS subsystem.

---

## **L — Lifecycle & Legibility**

### **Legibility Floor**  
The minimum clarity required for a module to be safely interpreted. If violated, SIAP vetoes the pin.

### **Lifecycle (TPS)**  
The seven‑stage process a module follows from proposal to activation or retirement.

---

## **M — Map Pin & Modules**

### **Map Pin**  
The structural anchor inside the conceptual architecture. Paired with a SIAP Pin.

### **Module**  
Any conceptual component of UMA that may eventually be anchored.

---

## **P — Pacing & Pins**

### **Pacing Alignment**  
Ensures modules are anchored in the correct order. Prevents premature cosmic anchoring.

### **Pin Pair**  
The combined Map Pin + SIAP Pin that stabilizes a module.

### **Pin Veto**  
A SIAP decision preventing anchoring due to unmet requirements.

---

## **R — Requirements & Render Integrity**

### **Render Integrity**  
The ability of a module to maintain shape, spacing, and clarity across viewports (GitHub Pages, mobile, etc.).

### **Requirements (TPS)**  
The threshold criteria a module must meet before anchoring.

---

## **S — SIAP & Stability**

### **SIAP**  
System Integrity Alignment Protocol. Governs anchoring, pacing, safety, and stability.

### **Stability**  
A module’s ability to remain consistent across contexts, devices, and conceptual layers.

---

## **T — TPS & Traversal**

### **TPS**  
Two‑Pin System. The dual‑anchor model governing UMA’s stability.

### **Thoroughfare**  
The traversal layer of UMA. Not yet eligible for anchoring.

---

## **V — Veto & Viewport**

### **Viewport Collapse**  
When a module compresses or bunches due to rendering constraints. A common cause of SIAP veto.

### **Veto (SIAP)**  
A formal rejection of a proposed pin. Logged in the SIAP ledger.
