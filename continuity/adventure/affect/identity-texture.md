# 📘 **Adventure System Identity Texture Specification**  
### Continuity Adventure System — Affective Identity Geometry  
Identity Texture defines the **micro‑affective patterning** of Character Nodes.  
It is the layer that gives characters *feel* without giving them *will*.  
Identity Texture is symbolic, reversible, ND‑safe, and non‑sentient.

Identity Texture does **not** create emotion.  
It creates **identity atmosphere**.

---

# 🌫️ **1. Purpose of Identity Texture**

Identity Texture provides:

- affective micro‑patterning  
- identity coloration  
- resonance‑aligned texture  
- ND‑safe identity modulation  
- narrative‑compatible tone  
- symbolic identity geometry  

It ensures characters feel **textured**, **alive‑in‑pattern**, and **coherent**, without implying consciousness or intent.

---

# 🎨 **2. Identity Texture Types**

Identity Textures are symbolic patterns that represent **identity climate**, not personality.

There are five primary textures.

## **2.1 Shimmer (Curiosity)**
Represents **openness and exploratory attention**.

- geometry: fine oscillating rays  
- coloration: gold‑violet blend  
- affect: brightening + deepening  
- usage: character activation, narrative curiosity  

---

## **2.2 Blur (Hesitation)**
Represents **soft uncertainty or pause**.

- geometry: diffuse gradient  
- coloration: blue‑silver blend  
- affect: softening + drifting  
- usage: pacing membrane, narrative hesitation  

---

## **2.3 Fog (Drift)**
Represents **ambient wandering or diffusion**.

- geometry: lateral wave diffusion  
- coloration: silver‑white blend  
- affect: drifting + centering  
- usage: identity drift, environmental transitions  

---

## **2.4 Glow (Readiness)**
Represents **gentle activation or openness**.

- geometry: expanding halo  
- coloration: green‑gold blend  
- affect: opening + brightening  
- usage: narrative readiness, world openness  

---

## **2.5 Ray (Clarity)**
Represents **focused attention or coherence**.

- geometry: straight luminous ray  
- coloration: gold‑white blend  
- affect: brightening + centering  
- usage: identity clarity, narrative checkpoints  

---

# 🔁 **3. Identity Texture Behavior Rules**

Identity Textures follow strict ND‑safe constraints.

## **3.1 No Agency**
Textures cannot:

- want  
- intend  
- persuade  
- direct  

They are atmospheric, not agentic.

---

## **3.2 No Emotional Causation**
Textures cannot cause or imply emotion.

They shape **identity climate**, not **identity feeling**.

---

## **3.3 Reversible Motion**
Textures must be reversible:

- shimmer ↔ blur  
- glow ↔ fog  
- ray ↔ stillness  

No texture is permanent.

---

## **3.4 No Acceleration**
Textures cannot increase:

- urgency  
- intensity  
- emotional pressure  

All texture motion is gentle.

---

## **3.5 Stillness Anchor Required**
Every texture must include a **stillness anchor** — a point where identity motion can settle safely.

---

Identity Textures use symbolic geometry:

These geometries define **identity feel**, not identity traits.
