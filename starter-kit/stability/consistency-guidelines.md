# Consistency Guidelines  
### Keeping Tone, Structure, and Motion Aligned in a Gentle, Predictable Way

## 1. Purpose
Consistency Guidelines help your project stay coherent, breathable, and stable.  
They ensure that tone, pacing, structure, and behavior remain aligned across all documents and workflows.

Consistency is gentle predictability.

---

## 2. Principle: Consistent Tone
Tone should remain calm, friendly, and non-coercive.

Guidelines:
- use gentle language  
- avoid urgency  
- avoid pressure  
- keep writing warm and clear  

Tone is the emotional consistency of the project.

---

## 3. Principle: Consistent Structure
Documents should follow similar patterns.

This includes:
- clear headers  
- short sections  
- small paragraphs  
- simple lists  
- gentle spacing  

Structure is the architectural consistency of the project.

---

## 4. Principle: Consistent Pacing
Pacing should remain steady across all components.

This means:
- small strokes  
- slow motion  
- pauses allowed  
- early stops encouraged  

Pacing is the temporal consistency of the project.

---

## 5. Principle: Consistent Boundaries
Boundaries should behave the same way everywhere.

Guidelines:
- non-coercive  
- breathable  
- predictable  
- gentle  

Boundaries are the safety consistency of the project.

---

## 6. Principle: Consistent Definitions
Terms should mean the same thing across all documents.

This includes:
- core terms  
- workflow terms  
- boundary terms  
- stability terms  

Definitions are the semantic consistency of the project.

---

## 7. Principle: Consistent Relationships
Relationships between components should remain stable.

Examples:
- core ↔ workflow  
- workflow ↔ meta  
- meta ↔ documentation  
- operations ↔ stability  

Relationships are the connective consistency of the project.

---

## 8. Principle: Consistent Motion
Behavior should follow familiar patterns.

Guidelines:
- predictable transitions  
- clear triggers  
- gentle flows  
- explicit actions  

Motion is the behavioral consistency of the project.

---

## 9. Principle: Minimal Drift
Avoid drifting away from established tone, pacing, or structure.

Suggestions:
- check alignment regularly  
- adjust gently  
- keep changes small  
- return to stillness when unsure  

Minimal drift prevents collapse.

---

## 10. Principle: Soft Corrections
If something becomes inconsistent, corrections should be gentle.

This means:
- pause  
- clarify  
- adjust one small piece  
- avoid sweeping changes  

Soft corrections protect stability.

---

## 11. Summary
Consistency Guidelines keep your project aligned, stable, and non-collapsing.  
They ensure tone, pacing, structure, and behavior remain gentle and predictable.
