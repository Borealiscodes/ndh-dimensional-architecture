## **II. Operator Classes**
Mythic operators fall into three structural classes:

### **1. Stability Operators (\(\mathcal{M}_{stab}\))**
These maintain coherence.

- **Continuity Operator \(K\)**  
  Mathematical origin: span contraction  
  Mythic function: restore coherence, bind meaning  
  \[
  K(s) \rightarrow s_{\text{coherent}}
  \]

- **Holonomy Operator \(H\)**  
  Mathematical origin: path‑dependent invariance  
  Mythic function: preserve meaning across narrative loops  
  \[
  H(\gamma) = \text{meaning}_{start} = \text{meaning}_{end}
  \]

### **2. Fracture Operators (\(\mathcal{M}_{frac}\))**
These introduce controlled instability.

- **Collapse Operator \(C\)**  
  Mathematical origin: recursion‑fracture  
  Mythic function: introduce crisis, fracture, or destabilization  
  \[
  C(s) \rightarrow s_{\text{fractured}}
  \]

### **3. Relation Operators (\(\mathcal{M}_{rel}\))**
These create adjacency and meaning‑links.

- **Adjacency Operator \(A\)**  
  Mathematical origin: cyclic neighbor averaging  
  Mythic function: create meaningful relationships  
  \[
  A(x,y) = \text{linked}(x,y)
  \]
