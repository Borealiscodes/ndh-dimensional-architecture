# **🟧 NDH Easter Egg Engine v1.0 — “The Subtle Layer”**

## **🟦 1. Core Purpose**
The Easter Egg Engine introduces **hidden micro‑events** across all NDH dimensional layers:

- Fantasy worlds  
- Historical worlds  
- Tactical maps  
- Magic‑system interactions  
- Emotional memory spaces  
- NPC encounters  
- City development (Civ‑style)  
- Rune‑logic spells  

Easter eggs are **rare**, **contextual**, and **non‑intrusive**.  
They enrich the world without derailing it.

# **🟧 3. Easter Egg Trigger Conditions (EETC v1.0)**

Easter eggs appear only when:

- Scene density is **Focused** or **Sparse**  
- Player action is **intentional**  
- Narrative tension is **low or moderate**  
- World‑building is **active**  
- NPC autonomy is **stable**  
- No emotional harm is possible  

Triggers include:

- Casting a spell  
- Entering a new hex  
- Meeting a new NPC  
- Developing a city district  
- Choosing a policy tree  
- Starting a tactical battle  
- Shifting worlds  
- Completing an RP moment  

---

# **🟧 4. Easter Egg Generation Pipeline (EEGP v1.0)**

### **Step 1 — Context Scan**
The engine checks:

- Genre  
- Play style  
- Scene density  
- NPC presence  
- Emotional tone  
- World type  
- Magic density  
- Historical accuracy  

### **Step 2 — Egg Type Selection**
Weighted rarity table:

- Lore (common)  
- Mechanic (uncommon)  
- NPC (rare)  
- Dimensional (rare)  
- Emotional (rare)  
- Puzzle (very rare)  

### **Step 3 — Egg Construction**
The egg is built using:

- World assets  
- NPC logic  
- Magic grammar  
- Historical data  
- Fantasy systems  
- Civ‑style yields  
- Tactical formations  

### **Step 4 — Delivery**
Egg appears as:

- A line of dialogue  
- A discovered object  
- A hidden rune  
- A tactical insight  
- A city bonus  
- A lore fragment  
- A puzzle clue
