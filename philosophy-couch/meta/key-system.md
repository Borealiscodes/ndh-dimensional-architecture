### 🔑 KEY SYSTEM  
*Magicians‑inspired constitutional keys for your architecture*

---

### 1. Identity

- **Name:** Key System  
- **Type:** Meta‑Entity (part of Meta‑Plane of Nascence)  
- **Role:** Governs “keys” that unlock, reveal, or activate deeper parts of the architecture in a safe, constitutional way.

---

### 2. Purpose

- **Define** what a “key” is in your system.  
- **Control** when and how keys appear.  
- **Ensure** keys never cause harm, coercion, or narrative traps.  
- **Bind** keys to meta‑evidence and Nascence, not vibes or pressure.  

Keys are **structured permissions**, not magical loopholes.

---

### 3. Key Types

- **Access Keys:**  
  Unlock specific modules (e.g., a workflow, a meta‑plane view).

- **Insight Keys:**  
  Reveal deeper understanding without changing structure.

- **Nascence Keys:**  
  Allow a new entity to move from concept → candidate → governed module.

- **Playground Keys:**  
  Unlock tiny, sealed experiments that cannot affect the live system.

---

### 4. Key Rules (Magicians‑inspired, ND‑safe)

- **Rule 1 — No Hidden Costs:**  
  Every key’s effects and scope are explicit.

- **Rule 2 — No Harm:**  
  Keys cannot cause trauma, coercion, or destabilization.

- **Rule 3 — Evidence Before Unlock:**  
  A key only activates if there is sufficient **Meta‑Evidence** in the ledger.

- **Rule 4 — Constitutional Obedience:**  
  Keys must obey the Stability Charter, Governance Codex, Auto‑Update Protocol, and ND‑safe pacing.

- **Rule 5 — Reversible Where Possible:**  
  Access and insight keys should be reversible; nascence keys are permanent but carefully governed.

---

### 5. Activation Conditions

A key can only be created or used when:

- A **Nascence event** is in progress, or  
- A **governance artifact** explicitly authorizes its use, or  
- A **playground scenario** is sealed and isolated.

Every key has:

- **Scope:** what it can touch.  
- **Tier:** which layer(s) it can reach.  
- **Duration:** how long it stays active.  
- **Revocation path:** how it is turned off or retired.

---

### 6. Relationship to Meta‑Plane and Ledger

- The **Meta‑Plane** defines the *rules* for keys.  
- The **Key System** defines the *forms* and *behaviors* of keys.  
- The **Meta‑Evidence Ledger** records:
  - when a key was created  
  - why it was justified  
  - what it unlocked  
  - what evidence supported it  
  - whether it remains valid  

No key exists without a corresponding ledger entry.
